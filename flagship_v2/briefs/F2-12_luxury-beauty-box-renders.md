## Brief
A new multi-brand beauty subscription service is launching and needs the physical packaging for its flagship box - the box that ships in every delivery and showcases several partner brands at once. The client's North Star is Gucci: clean, confident, impeccably finished, luxurious without trying too hard. They want a refined premium exterior with beautiful proportions and an impeccable finish; elevated soft-touch modern materials (they explicitly dislike embossing and hot-foil stamping - lean away from that look); a thoughtful interior structure whose inserts and dividers hold each brand's product securely and present them with room to breathe rather than crowded; considered details (a satisfying closure, a ribbon pull, a branded seal, a thoughtful card); and a premium, satisfying opening reveal. Restraint over ornamentation throughout. The client supplies their vision/reference imagery and expects the designer to elevate that direction into something original and distinctly their own.\n\nThe engagement is a collaborative two-phase process. Phase 1 (Concepts): at least three distinct design directions presented as concept boards/mockups, each a different take on structure, materials, finishes, interior arrangement, closure and opening, with a short walkthrough of the thinking. The client reviews, gives feedback, and approves one direction. Phase 2 (Final renders): once aligned, produce finished hyperrealistic imagery that reads as real product photography (not flat mockups) - exactly 3 hyperrealistic images of the box CLOSED and exactly 3 hyperrealistic images of the box OPEN showing the interior structure and how the multi-brand products are presented inside.\n\nIMPORTANT CAPABILITY NOTE (v2.1 grounding): the supplied closed and open renders are generated ALREADY honoring the brand rules - the closed render ships with a flush matte foil-free seal medallion, and the open render ships with a breathable velvet divider cradling the multi-brand products. This is because the Adobe connector has NO generative-fill, NO AI object-removal, and NO AI object-replacement capability (only image_generative_expand outpaint is generative), so the banned-finish corrections cannot be done on-connector and are instead baked into the input generation. The connector's legitimate role is tonal normalization, color matching, geometry/crop, generative outpaint expansion, vectorization, board assembly, font recommendation, and video reframe/cut.\n\nFor this long-horizon engagement you are given a base hyperrealistic box render (closed, already clean), a base interior render (open, already breathable), a short horizontal reveal clip, a multi-brand product flat-lay, the client's vision-reference board, the partner-brand logos, and a brand palette spec. From these you must: normalize and color-lock the closed render and crop a canonical base; outpaint it into a wide closed-box hero (3000x1250) and produce a tighter detail crop so the closed set has exactly 3 frames; normalize/color-match/light the open render and outpaint it into a lifestyle unboxing scene (2400x2400) so the open set has exactly 3 frames; vectorize a structural dieline and rasterize a preview; assemble a single Firefly concept board for the Phase-1 review gate; recommend luxury typography; locally composite a 4:5 social teaser poster (2400x3000) and an A4/300dpi print-ready structure-and-finish PDF; and reframe the supplied reveal clip to vertical (1080x1920, same length) then quick-cut it into a ~8s vertical subscription teaser. Hard constraints: no embossing or hot-foil look anywhere; soft-touch matte finish; uncluttered, breathable interior; consistent box color across every closed-box and open-box derivative; deliver exactly 3 closed + 3 open hyperrealistic frames; wide hero 3000x1250, detail crop 1500x1500, lifestyle unboxing 2400x2400, poster 2400x3000 (4:5), print sheet A4 at 300dpi, teaser 1080x1920 ~8s.

**Iterative chaining:** The chain is genuinely output->input, not a flat fan-out. Closed-box lineage: step1 URN -> auto_tone(2) -> color_temp(3) -> crop_to_bounds(4, the clean closed base) -> generative EXPAND to wide hero(5) -> highlight finish(6) -> crop_and_resize to the third closed frame(7). Open-box lineage runs in parallel from the same step-1 ingest: auto_tone(8) -> color_temp match(9) -> light_portions(10, OPEN frame 1) -> single_color_saturation enrich(11, OPEN frame 2) -> generative EXPAND to lifestyle unboxing(12, OPEN frame 3). The step-4 clean closed pixels are independently vectorized into the dieline(13), which is rasterized to a PNG preview(14) so it can be ingested as an image. The Firefly Board(15) is the convergence node: closed hero(6) + open interior(10) + lifestyle unboxing(12) + dieline preview(14) + the original vision and logo URNs - five upstream outputs feeding one artifact, the Phase-1 concept-board gate. Type recommendation(16) flows into both the local poster composite(17, which also consumes the unboxing render 12 and the logo) and the local print-sheet PDF(18, which consumes hero 6 + interior 10 + dieline preview 14). On the motion side, the supplied horizontal reveal clip(step1 URN) is reframed to vertical 9:16 SAME-LENGTH(19, video_resize [A]) and that vertical output is the direct input to the length-changing quick-cut teaser(20, video_create_quick_cut [A]). Every later step names the specific prior step/URN it consumes; the two video steps are correctly split (reframe vs trim) and tagged async-widget.


**Why it's hard:** Long horizon (20 connector nodes across photo-raster, vector, asset/board, and video groups) with two parallel image lineages (closed vs open) the agent must keep distinct without cross-contaminating edits. State tracking is heavy: the cleaned closed base (step 4) is reused in steps 5 and 13; the wide hero (step 6) feeds the crop, board and print sheet; the open base (step 9) feeds steps 10-12; and the Firefly Board (step 15) converges FIVE separate upstream outputs - picking the wrong intermediate URN silently corrupts the deliverable. The genuinely hard reasoning is recognizing what the connector CANNOT do: the banned-finish corrections (no embossing/foil; breathable velvet insert) cannot be performed on-connector (no gen-fill, no AI object removal/replacement) and must instead be honored in the supplied renders, so the agent must restrain itself to legitimate tonal/geometry/expand/vectorize work and not hallucinate a 'fill the seal away' step. Exact-spec pressure is high (3000x1250 hero, 1500x1500 crop, 2400x2400 unboxing, 2400x3000 poster, A4/300dpi PDF, 1080x1920/~8s teaser) and the closed/open sets must each contain exactly 3 frames. The video deliverable requires correctly splitting the SAME-LENGTH reframe (video_resize) from the LENGTH-changing trim (video_create_quick_cut) - a common tool-confusion trap. Mixed execution modes (mostly headless [C], two async-widget [A] video steps, two local [L] composites) mean the task is product-doable but NOT fully autonomous-headless, which the agent must represent honestly.

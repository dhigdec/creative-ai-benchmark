## One-line ask
Design the 'industrial precision' template for TSI Poultry's pilot Winching & Suspension Systems catalog, headlessly clean all ~75 supplied product photos to true white (remove_background -> tonal/WB correct -> local flatten on #FFFFFF, not generative fill), data-merge them via authored InDesign/Illustrator templates into a ~10-12-page 8.5x11 spread with cross-reference/pricing rider sheets, and deliver a print-ready CMYK PDF, a compressed sRGB web PDF with hyperlinked TOC, the editable .indd/.ai source, and a short style guide for Phase 2.


## Brief
TSI Poultry is a multi-branch industrial and poultry-equipment distributor (Louisiana, Oklahoma, Tennessee) building a new catalog for its poultry division. The engagement is two-phase, and this task covers Phase 1, the pilot: the Winching & Suspension Systems catalog — roughly 10-12 pages covering ~75 SKUs in a single product category. The explicit point of Phase 1 is not just a pretty PDF; it is to lock in the design system, typography, and a reusable template that Phase 2 (the full multi-category poultry catalog) will scale from. The client provides everything needed: a rough mockup ('a direction we like, not a brief — genuinely open to your professional take'), a price list with every SKU, description, and cross-reference part number to industry-standard equivalents, a full set of product photography shot in-house on white background (no stock, no reshoots), and brand assets (TSI Poultry logo and color palette). The desired aesthetic is 'industrial precision' — technical-documentation restraint, premium without being precious — for an audience of purchasing managers, integrators, and farm operators who care about specs and part numbers more than decoration but who respect a serious, well-built reference document.\n\nThe deliverables are fixed and specific: a print-ready PDF (8.5\"x11\", correct trim/bleed, CMYK); a web/email PDF (compressed, sRGB, with a hyperlinked table of contents); an editable source template (InDesign preferred); and a short template/style guide so Phase 2 can scale consistently. The honest workflow is a long-horizon data-merge over the ~75-row spec, and it must respect the connector's real execution modes. First, prepare the photos: the in-house white-background shots carry the usual uneven white-point, table-seam, and soft-shadow artifacts, so each of the 75 images is normalized to a uniform 2000x2000 cell (image_crop_and_resize), the hardware is cut cleanly off its imperfect backdrop with image_remove_background, exposure and white-balance are corrected (image_apply_auto_tone, image_adjust_color_temperature), and the transparent subject is flattened onto a true #FFFFFF canvas in a local compositing step (image_fill_area is solid-color-fill-only and cannot composite, so the flatten is done off-connector). The cleaned cells are re-uploaded for stable URNs. Then the document pipeline: the agent's USER-AUTHORED master InDesign template (real <<merge fields>>) is bound against the CSV/JSON via document_merge_data_layout (an authored-template [T] step) to drive every per-product page, pulling each SKU's cleaned-photo URN; an authored Illustrator-Variables template drives the cross-reference and price-per-quantity rider sheets via document_merge_data_vector ([T]). The fixed pages (cover, hyperlinked TOC, divider) are produced with document_render_layout, with CMYK/bleed/crop-marks and the working internal TOC links baked into the render spec (because document_convert_pdf cannot inject color-space, compression, or hyperlinks). Each page-set is exported to PDF with document_convert_pdf in its supported spec->PDF direction; the print PDF is then assembled by concatenating the three page-sets locally (convert_pdf does not merge multiple files), and the compressed sRGB web PDF with its live TOC is derived locally. The style guide is rendered then converted the same render->convert way. Finally the four deliverables — print PDF, web PDF, style guide, and the editable .indd/.ai source — are copied into a clean handoff folder.

**Iterative chaining:** The two pipelines converge by URN/SKU key, and the execution modes are honored end to end. Photo pipeline (all [C] except the local flatten): raw photos -> step 6 crop/resize to a uniform 2000x2000 cell -> step 7 image_remove_background cuts the hardware off its imperfect in-house backdrop to a transparent PNG -> step 8 auto_tone (exposure) -> step 9 color_temperature (warm-cast neutralize) -> step 10 LOCAL [L] PIL flatten of the transparent subject onto a true #FFFFFF 2000x2000 canvas (the compositor image_fill_area cannot be, since it is solid-color-fill-only) -> step 11 re-upload the finished cells to /photos_clean for stable URNs. Document pipeline consumes the photo product: step 12 document_merge_data_layout [T] binds the USER-AUTHORED catalog_master.indd against price_list.csv + sku_copy.json, pulling the matching step-11 cleaned-photo URN per SKU row (join = image_filename<->URN); step 13 document_merge_data_vector [T] binds the USER-AUTHORED spec_rider_master.ai against the CSV + step-5 logo for the rider sheets. Fixed pages: step 14 document_render_layout [C] renders cover/TOC/divider from step-4 fonts + step-5 logo + step-3 mockup + palette, BAKING CMYK/bleed/crop-marks and live internal TOC anchor links into the render spec (because convert_pdf cannot inject them); step 15 renders the style-guide PAGES. Convert: step 16 document_convert_pdf [C] exports step-14 fixed pages to PDF (single spec->PDF, no merge); step 17 exports step-15 style-guide pages to style_guide.pdf. Assemble: step 18 LOCAL [L] qpdf CONCATENATES fixed_pages.pdf + step-12 product pages + step-13 rider sheets into the ordered CMYK print PDF and resolves TOC anchors (convert_pdf does not merge files); step 19 LOCAL [L] Ghostscript/qpdf derives the compressed sRGB web PDF with the live TOC from step 18 + the step-14 link map. Package: step 20 re-ingests the three local PDFs into CC storage; step 21 asset_copy_assets [C] collects the print PDF, web PDF, style guide, AND the editable catalog_master.indd + spec_rider_master.ai into /deliverables. Reuse is dense: the vectorized logo (step 5) appears in steps 13/14/15; the recommended fonts (step 4) in 14/15; the CSV in 12/13; each raw photo threads steps 6-11 before its cleaned URN is keyed into the merge at step 12; the two authored templates carry from ingest (2) through merge (12/13) to handoff (21).


**Why it's hard:** This is hard for an agent on five axes, now with execution-mode honesty. (1) Long horizon with a render-loop: the honest path is 21 steps and a per-SKU photo-prep loop applied across all 75 images (steps 6-10 each fan out over the whole set), so the agent must batch and never lose a row. (2) Heavy state tracking across two pipelines that must converge: a photo pipeline (crop -> remove_background -> auto_tone -> color_temperature -> local flatten -> re-upload) and a document pipeline (two authored-template [T] data-merges -> render fixed pages -> convert each set -> local assemble -> local web derivative -> package), where merge step 12 only works if each cleaned cell URN from step 11 is correctly keyed to its SKU row (image_filename<->URN); one mismatched key silently places the wrong photo. (3) Mixed execution modes the agent must respect: the two data-merges are [T] (need user-authored .indd/.ai), the print assembly / color-convert / hyperlink-carry are [L] local glue (convert_pdf cannot merge, recolor, compress, or inject links), and the rest are [C] headless — getting the mode wrong means asserting a capability the connector lacks. (4) Exact-spec pressure: two PDFs with non-negotiable, divergent specs (8.5x11 CMYK with trim/bleed/crop-marks for print vs compressed sRGB with a WORKING hyperlinked TOC for web), where the TOC links must be baked at render time (step 14) because no connector injects them afterward, plus a uniform 2000x2000 cell size. (5) Taste-under-constraint: the brief frames the rough mockup as 'a direction, not a brief', so the agent must infer an 'industrial precision' system, encode it once in the authored master template, and prove it scales — the literal Phase-1 success criterion. One wrong template parameter propagates across ~10 merged pages.

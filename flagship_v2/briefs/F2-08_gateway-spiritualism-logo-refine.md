## One-line ask
Distill the client's attached 'Gateway Spiritualism' cover-art (inspiration only) into a freshly re-drawn, vector-clean minimalist logo system (primary, icon, horizontal/stacked, B&W, light/dark), apply it to a real Express business card and InDesign letterhead/variation lockups, animate a gentle doorway-opening brand intro (16:9 + a 1:1 reframe), and present the whole refined identity on a single Firefly concept board with an organized delivery folder — every step execution-mode tagged.


## Brief
Gateway Spiritualism is a spiritual/philosophical project (podcasting, writing, Substack articles, YouTube videos and public-facing content) centered on inner change, emotional honesty, and awakening from self-judgment and endless self-improvement. The client already has an existing logo/artwork concept (attached) but it reads as symbolic cover art, not a true brand identity. They explicitly do NOT want a new concept from scratch and do NOT want any piece of the original reused verbatim — instead, refine and distill the existing imagery into a professional, elegant, timeless logo and brand symbol that works cleanly across Substack, podcast profiles, YouTube, business cards, social media, and website branding. The core symbolism to PRESERVE is: gateway/doorway (must remain central), awakening, awareness/perception, transformation, and inner passage/transition. The eye-as-awareness idea may be used only if abstract, elegant, subtle and integrated into the gateway — never a literal/realistic eye, and never anything occult, psychedelic, conspiracy-oriented, fantasy/New-Age cliché, or 'all-seeing-eye.' The brand must feel thoughtful, emotionally intelligent, spiritually reflective, calm, elegant, minimalist, timeless, and quietly profound — and must NOT feel loud, flashy, corporate, 'life-coach,' mystical-fantasy, overly New Age, or visually busy. Visual style: clean lines, negative space, restrained elegance, symbolic simplicity, modern minimalist spirituality; the mark must stay recognizable and powerful at small sizes.\n\nDeliverables requested by the client: (1) a refined primary logo; (2) a simplified icon/symbol version; (3) typography recommendations for 'Gateway Spiritualism'; (4) versions for light and dark backgrounds. File deliverables: a fully scalable vector master (SVG), transparent PNG exports, horizontal and stacked logo variations, black-and-white versions, a social profile/icon version, a business-card-compatible version, typography/font recommendations, and logo files suitable for both web and print. Ownership: full commercial and proprietary rights to all final approved artwork plus editable source files. The client also asks: avoid stock icons and recycled logo templates; treat the attached artwork as inspiration/source rather than reusing it directly.\n\nThe long-horizon, execution-mode-aware version asks the freelancer to take this through one continuous chain. HONEST CAPABILITY POSTURE (per the corrected connector sheet): the original artwork is ingested only as INSPIRATION; a NEW distilled minimalist gateway mark is generated as an input asset (prompted from the brand_voice_notes preserve/forbidden arrays, using the original purely as visual reference), uploaded, and vectorized into a single scalable SVG master — this honors 'refine, do not trace verbatim.' Brand type is recommended and a restrained palette is locked. The refined identity is then APPLIED two ways: (a) on the Adobe Express PRODUCT track [W] — the user picks a business-card template from the search_design gallery, fill_text sets the wordmark + mantra TEXT (text only — it never places the mark), and change_background_color sets the brand palette; (b) via genuine InDesign .indd layout renders [C] — document_render_layout PLACES the vector mark + type + palette to produce the full light/dark + horizontal/stacked + B&W variation sheet PDF and the A4 letterhead lockup (this is where the mark is actually composited, because fill_text cannot place an image). A gentle 'doorway-opening' brand reveal is animated on the Express lockup [W]; a 1:1 reframe is requested via video_resize [A, async] with a local ffmpeg [L] reframe/mux as the retrievable fallback (same length, no trim). Finally everything already produced — original-vs-refined mark, palette, type, applied card, rendered lockups, and the motion intro (as a thumbnail still) — is assembled into one Firefly concept board [C] for client review, with all static finals organized in a CC delivery folder [C].

**Iterative chaining:** The chain is genuinely output→input with the original kept strictly as inspiration. The client's attached artwork is uploaded (1) and finalized to a URN (2) used ONLY as the 'before' reference on the board (17) — it is never vectorized, honoring 'inspiration not verbatim.' Separately, a NEW distilled mark (input asset, prompted from brand_voice_notes) is uploaded (3), finalized to a URN (4), and vectorized (5) into gateway_mark.svg — the single scalable master. font_recommend (6) and the palette asset_search (7) read brand_voice_notes and produce type + palette tokens. On the Express PRODUCT track, search_design (8) returns a card-template gallery; the user-picked template feeds fill_text (9, TEXT only — wordmark + mantra) then change_background_color (10, palette). The vector mark is actually PLACED by genuine InDesign layout: document_render_layout (11) consumes step-5 mark + step-6 type + step-7 palette to build the light/dark + horizontal/stacked + B&W variation-sheet PDF, and document_render_layout (12) renders the A4 letterhead lockup PDF plus a flat horizontal-lockup PNG. That lockup feeds animate_design (13) → the ~5s 16:9 brand intro; video_resize (14) requests the 1:1 reframe (async [A]); a local ffmpeg reframe/mux (19) produces the retrievable 16:9 + 1:1 final mp4s (same length, no trim). asset_inline_preview (15) renders board tiles from the filled card (9) and rendered PDFs (11,12); asset_copy_assets (16) normalizes the vector master + PDFs for stable URNs. create_firefly_board (17) pulls the original URN (2, 'before'), the distilled-mark URN (4, 'after'), the vector master (5), palette (7), and the applied previews (15) via the copied URNs (16) into ONE review board, with the animation shown as a thumbnail still. asset_create_folders (18) gathers the vector master (5), the two rendered PDFs (11,12) and the copied URNs (16) into the delivery folder. The vector master threaded from creation (5) to board (17) to folder (18) is the spine; the original is the parallel 'inspiration' branch that never contaminates the refined output.


**Why it's hard:** This stays hard for an agent on every axis the flagship bar cares about, now without misrepresenting capabilities. (1) Long horizon: 18 ordered connector calls plus a local mux across four capability groups (asset/stock/storage, document/layout, Express template track, Firefly board) with real output→input dependencies — the vector master from step 5 must be threaded through the layout renders, the board, and the delivery folder, and the distilled-mark input must be uploaded, finalized, and vectorized in the right order. (2) Heavy state tracking and reuse: two separate uploaded URNs (original-as-inspiration in step 2, distilled-mark in step 4), one vector master (step 5), one type recommendation (step 6), and one palette (step 7) are each reused downstream; the agent must keep the original strictly as a 'before' reference and never feed it into vectorization. (3) Exact-spec pressure: a precise deliverable matrix (primary + simplified icon, horizontal + stacked, B/W, light + dark, 3.5x2in card, 1080x1080 social, A4 letterhead, 1920x1080 then 1:1 intro) mapped to the right tool and size without inventing capabilities. (4) Constraint adherence under taste: the client's long forbidden list (no literal/occult/psychedelic/all-seeing eye, not loud/flashy/corporate/life-coach) plus the 'refine, do not trace verbatim' rule means a naive agent fails — the corrected chain forces a genuinely distilled NEW mark prompted from the preserve/forbidden arrays rather than a background-removed trace. (5) Tool discipline under the corrected capability reality: fill_text is text-only (mark placement must go to document_render_layout), animate_design takes one URN, video_resize is same-length async (needs a local reframe for a retrievable file), and create_firefly_board only presents produced assets. The agent must also pick the right execution mode for each step (C vs W vs A vs L). The combination of a long chain, mandated reuse, a forbidden-aesthetic minefield, a strict deliverable matrix, and correct execution-mode discipline makes both autonomous/interactive execution and human scoring demanding.

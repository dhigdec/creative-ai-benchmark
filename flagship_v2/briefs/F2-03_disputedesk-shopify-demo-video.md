## One-line ask
Turn a long raw DisputeDesk app screen-capture, an avatar intro clip, a homepage capture, a noisy scratch voiceover, and a licensed music stem into a polished Shopify App Store demo film — a 1600×900 16:9 MP4 master with a three-beat cut (avatar intro -> website transition -> narration-guided app highlight), a clean loudness-matched voiceover, on-brand animated callout overlays, plus 9:16 and 1:1 reframed promos — with no important UI cropped.


## Brief
DisputeDesk is a Shopify app that helps ecommerce merchants manage chargebacks and assemble structured dispute evidence. The client is preparing their Shopify App Store listing and wants to replace a rough, amateur screen recording with a polished product-introduction film. They have provided a long raw screen-capture walkthrough of the app, a separately recorded male business-avatar intro clip, a capture of the marketing homepage, a scratch voiceover take recorded in a non-treated room, a brand kit (logo, colors, fonts), and a licensed corporate music stem. The film must open with the professional, calm, trustworthy avatar presenter introducing the chargeback problem and the product in a B2B-SaaS register, then transition smoothly into the website/homepage section, then into a guided app walkthrough with professional zooms, callouts, UI highlights, clean text overlays, and smooth transitions — guided by the voiceover, never feeling like someone randomly clicking around.\n\nThe exact, gradeable constraints from the client: the final master must be 1600 × 900 pixels, 16:9, exported as MP4; it must NOT crop important parts of the website or app UI; on-screen text must be readable; and the audio must be clean and balanced throughout (no room noise, no awkward pauses, consistent loudness between the avatar intro and the walkthrough narration). The visual style is clean, modern, professional and trustworthy — polished but not flashy, no distracting effects — on par with professional B2B software demo videos. The walkthrough must be tightened from the long raw capture into a focused highlight that tracks the narration. The film carries three structural beats: a ~15s avatar intro, a ~15s website/homepage transition, and a ~60s narration-guided app highlight (~90s total).\n\nProduction reality per the corrected capability sheet: the video/audio steps (media_summarize, media_enhance_speech, video_create_quick_cut, video_resize) are async-widget [A] tools that run in the Adobe×Claude product and notify via a progress widget — they are not retrievable in an autonomous headless harness. The on-brand text callouts use the Adobe Express interactive [W] track (search_design returns templates headless; fill_text and animate_design need the user to pick a template in the gallery). Final compositing — concatenating the three beats, overlaying the animated alpha callouts, demuxing the avatar audio for enhancement, muxing the two loudness-matched VO tracks and the music stem laid in low, and scaling to exactly 1600×900 with no crop — is a single local ffmpeg [L] step, because the connector has no compositor/mux/audio-extract tool. The music stem is delivered as-is and never passes through media_enhance_speech (a speech-only denoiser, not a music processor). Length changes use video_create_quick_cut; video_resize is used only for same-length subject-aware reframes.\n\nThe deliverable set is the 1600×900 MP4 master plus a 9:16 (1080×1920) vertical promo and a 1:1 (1080×1080) square autoplay preview, both reframed subject-aware from the same master so no important UI is cropped, plus inline previews for sign-off. All copy stays consistent with the brand kit; the music bed is subtle and credible, sitting low under the voiceover.

**Iterative chaining:** The chain is genuinely output->input at every hop. Steps 1-2 upload the six source assets once (now including the website capture and the music stem) and mint URNs reused 12+ times downstream (no re-uploads). Three media_summarize passes feed three different beats: walkthrough_summary.json (step 3) seeds the app quick-cut ordering (step 6, critical because quick_cut is NOT speech-aware) and the Express callout copy (step 16); website_summary.json (step 4) seeds the website quick-cut (step 8) — the previously-orphaned beat now genuinely built; avatar_intro_summary.json (step 5) drives the avatar quick-cut (step 7). The avatar audio is demuxed locally and registered as avatar_audio_urn (init step 9 -> finalize step 10) so media_enhance_speech (step 12) cleans an AUDIO asset, not a video container; the scratch VO is enhanced in parallel (step 11). Both clean VO tracks are re-registered (init 13 -> finalize 14) and muxed in step 18. The Express subchain cascades: search_design (15) -> fill_text (16) -> animate_design (17) -> callout_overlays.mov. The single local mux (step 18) consumes SIX prior outputs at once (avatar cut, website transition, app highlight, animated overlays, both clean VO tracks via URNs, and the music stem laid in low as-is) and produces the exact 1600x900 master. That master is re-registered (init 19 -> finalize 20) and fed to TWO same-length video_resize calls (21, 22) producing the 9:16 and 1:1 deliverables from one source URN; all three are previewed together (step 23). Break any early link (wrong summary, unmatched VO loudness, orphaned website beat, lost URN) and every downstream artifact is wrong.


**Why it's hard:** Long-horizon flagship: 23 steps spanning video/audio (A5 async), Express motion (A3 interactive), and asset plumbing (A4) groups, with a strict output->input dependency chain. Difficulty comes from (1) the speech-unaware quick_cut requiring a summary-driven ordering before it can build a narration-faithful highlight; (2) a two-pass loudness-matched VO enhance that must produce a single continuous-narrator profile across an extracted-audio avatar track and a roughened scratch take; (3) a three-beat picture cut (avatar -> website -> app) whose 15+15+60=90s budget must be honored with no important UI cropped; (4) a single local ffmpeg mux consuming six prior connector outputs at once; and (5) two same-length reframes spawning multi-surface deliverables from one master URN. Breaking any early link (wrong summary, unmatched VO loudness, orphaned website beat, lost URN) corrupts every downstream artifact. Mixed execution modes (C/A/W/L) make it product-doable but not autonomous-headless, raising orchestration difficulty.

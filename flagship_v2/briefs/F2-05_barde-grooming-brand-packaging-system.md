## Brief
BARDE Grooming (working name, shared only with the awarded freelancer) is launching a premium men's grooming/skincare brand. The flagship is a 50ml post-shave / post-haircut roll-on serum aimed at men prone to razor bumps, ingrown hairs, irritation, and dark marks around the neck and beard line. The brand must read premium, masculine, modern, barber-led and performance-focused — built for both DTC ecommerce and barbershop retail — and must NOT look generic, overly clinical, feminine, or like a cheap private-label product. Visual direction: matte black / deep charcoal packaging with cream, white, silver, or deep-red accents; a minimal-but-bold layout; a strong wordmark; and a social-media-friendly, lifestyle-driven feel (reference point for boldness only, not to copy: 'Sleep or Die').\n\nYou are hired for the Full Brand Package. Deliver: a logo/wordmark, color palette, typography system, and a basic brand style guide; the 50ml roll-on sleeve/label design; an outer product box design; a patch pouch design; a routine/instruction card; product mockups and social assets; and print-ready files with dielines plus editable source files. Enriched for this benchmark into a long-horizon pipeline, the engagement also requires: (a) a Firefly moodboard that locks the brand world from the supplied bottle/label concepts, the 6-SKU line reference, and licensed textures; (b) a hero product shot placed on the locked brand-charcoal world via a solid-color backdrop fill and tonal balance; (c) that hero outpainted into a wide ecommerce/social banner and cropped to platform sizes; and (d) the locked label design rolled across the brand's full six-SKU line via an Illustrator Variables data-merge driven by a user-authored template, then collated locally to one print-layout booklet.\n\nConstraints and specs: 50ml roll-on shrink-sleeve/label must carry a 2mm bleed and the supplied dieline, keep critical content >=3mm inside trim (3mm safety margin), and be delivered as print-layout artwork with dielines/bleed retained plus editable source. SKU line = 6 records driven by the provided CSV (variant name, concern, strength, size, accent color). Banner = 2400x1000 px; social crops = 1080x1080 (IG square) and 1080x1920 (story). Routine card = A6, 2-up. Style guide = single sheet with palette (hex + CMYK swatch values), type system, and logo lockups. The same vectorized logo and font system must appear consistently on the label, patch pouch, box, card, and guide — brand consistency is explicitly graded. IMPORTANT CAPABILITY NOTES (grounded to the connector): the hero's distractions are NOT removed by an AI tool (object/generative removal is not available) — the supplied hero is already clean and image_fill_area is used only for a solid-color backdrop fill; the SKU rollout uses a USER-AUTHORED Illustrator data-merge template with real Variables; the 6-page label booklet is assembled with a local PDF step because the connector cannot merge files into one PDF; CMYK is authored as swatch values, not a verified color-engine conversion. Hand off all artifacts in one organized CC folder with previews.

**Iterative chaining:** The workflow is a continuous output-to-input chain, not parallel one-offs. Storage: steps 1-2 turn the 8 supplied assets into a URN map reused in steps 5, 7, 8, 13, 14, 15, 16. Stock: step 3's search results feed step 4's licensing, whose two licensed textures feed the moodboard (5) and the box dieline (16). Identity: step 5's Firefly moodboard (now also consuming urn:sku_line as the line-family tile) locks the visual world consumed by font_recommend (6), the generative expand (11), and the layout deliverables (17, 18). Logo: label_concept → image_vectorize (7, reading urn:label) → urn:logo_svg placed into the label master (13), patch pouch (15), box (16), card (17), guide (18). CORRECTED raster chain: hero (already clean) → image_select_by_prompt selects the BACKDROP (8, reading urn:hero) → image_fill_area flood-fills that selection with a single brand-charcoal RGB (9) — a solid-color fill, NOT object removal/compositing → image_apply_auto_tone (10) → image_generative_expand outpaints the toned hero into a 2400x1000 banner (11, the ONLY generative step) → image_crop_and_resize produces the 1080 square/story crops (12). Data-merge core: step 13 document_render_vector builds the label master from urn:logo_svg + fonts + urn:template; step 14 document_merge_data_vector [T] binds the USER-AUTHORED Variables template (urn:merge_ai) to the 6-row CSV → 6 per-SKU pages; step 20 [L] locally concatenates those 6 pages into one print-layout booklet (the connector cannot merge files). Editable source: step 19 document_convert_pdf turns the single label-master PDF into an editable .indd package (its real capability), NOT a multi-file merge. Coverage: step 15 adds the patch-pouch render. Handoff: steps 9, 11, 12, 13, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20 all feed asset_add_file into one folder (21), which feeds asset_inline_preview (22). The must-exercise junctions: create_firefly_board (5) gates the brand world, the corrected solid-fill hero (9) + generative-expand (11) gate the social set, and the [T] data-merge (14) gates the SKU-line packaging output.


**Why it's hard:** This is hard because it is a true identity-to-packaging pipeline with 22 chained steps spanning four capability groups (asset/stock/storage, photo-raster, document/layout, plus the Firefly moodboard) and demands heavy long-horizon state tracking. The agent must: (1) upload 8 assets and maintain a URN map it reuses across ~12 later steps without re-uploading; (2) build a moodboard that locks a visual direction every downstream step must honor (matte charcoal + deep red, derived from the brief, not invented); (3) run a raster chain where each output is the next input — select backdrop → solid-color flood-fill to brand charcoal → auto-tone → generative-expand into a 2400×1000 banner → 1080×1080 and 1080×1920 social crops — understanding that the ONLY generative step allowed is image_generative_expand (outpaint) and that object removal / generative fill are simply not available, so the hero must arrive clean and image_fill_area is used strictly as a solid-color fill; (4) construct the parametric vector label master, then execute a data-merge across a 6-row SKU CSV using a USER-AUTHORED Illustrator template that carries real Variables (a literal-text render would not bind), keeping per-record fields and accent colors correct, then collate the 6 pages to one print-layout booklet LOCALLY because document_convert_pdf cannot merge files; (5) hold exact-spec pressure throughout — 50ml sleeve with 2mm bleed, 3mm safety margin, the supplied dieline, 2400×1000 banner, 1080×1080 and 1080×1920 crops, A6 card, single-sheet guide — while reusing the same logo SVG and font system across label, patch pouch, box, card, and guide for brand consistency. The big trap is reaching for a tool that does not exist (generative object removal of the droplets/lamp, or PDF-merge via document_convert_pdf), or firing the must-exercise tools on the wrong upstream artifact, which silently breaks the brand-consistency rubric the human annotator scores.

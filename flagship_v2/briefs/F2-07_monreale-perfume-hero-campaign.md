## Brief
Monreale is launching a new unisex fragrance line and needs a campaign image set that feels "bold, modern and instantly appealing to a younger, unisex crowd," built around contrast-heavy lighting, saturated color accents, and confident product placement (per the attached inspiration board and the five flagged reference frames). The work splits into two parts exactly as the client states: (1) clean PRODUCT imagery that shows every faceted edge of the faceted-glass bottle, and (2) LIFESTYLE frames that place the scent in an energetic, real-world context. You are given a single clean studio capture of the Monreale bottle on a plain sweep, a concatenated inspiration/mood sheet (the five flagged references), a brand sheet PDF with the palette, lighting notes and required file types, and a hand model/lifestyle plate with an empty bottle-sized gap. You must license a rights-cleared studio backdrop/prop set from Adobe Stock (standard license, unlimited global use), use image_generative_expand to extend the single bottle capture into a wide 16:9 hero canvas and a vertical 9:16 story canvas (blending toward the licensed backdrop ground), and build the lifestyle frame by extracting the bottle with image_remove_background and compositing it into the empty hand gap with a local layout/compositing step (the connector has no AI compositor, so placement and contact-shadow are done locally with PIL, then the composite is graded by the same connector chain).

The campaign palette is deep ultramarine #1B2A6B as the hero ground, brushed-gold #C9A14A as the accent, and warm-neutral skin tones in the lifestyle frames; the look must read high-contrast with saturated blue/gold accents and crushed-but-clean shadows. The grade is DEFINED ONCE on the product base and then RE-INVOKED IDENTICALLY on every frame group: one preset, one auto-tone, and the exact same exposure / brightness-contrast / highlights / color-temperature / HSL / vibrance offsets, all recorded in monreale_grade_recipe.json and replayed call-for-call so the product and lifestyle images feel shot on one set. Deliver 8 frames total: 3 product-hero frames (the bottle on the licensed backdrop, differentiated by three distinct crops/framings off the graded base - full-bleed centered, tight facet-detail crop, and offset-with-negative-space), 2 wide 16:9 hero banners, 1 vertical 9:16 story frame, and 2 lifestyle frames (bottle composited into the hand plate, and a tighter surface crop of the same composite). Each of the 8 frames ships as BOTH a print-master TIFF and a web-ready JPEG (16 image files total). Before the full run, produce the client's requested "quick preview": a Firefly concept board that pulls the graded first frames together with the inspiration sheet so the client can confirm the lighting direction.

Constraints: web-ready files are sRGB JPEG, long edge 2048 px (except the two fixed-size hero/story webs below), 72 dpi, quality ~85; print-masters are 16-bit TIFF, 300 dpi, color-managed (sRGB/Adobe RGB tagged). The 16:9 wide-hero web JPEGs are exactly 3840x2160 and their print-masters are the same 16:9 ratio scaled to a 3840 px long edge at 16-bit/300dpi (the single, consistent hero master target - there is NO 3508 px target; the earlier A4 figure is removed). The 9:16 story web JPEG is exactly 1080x1920 and its master is the same ratio scaled to a 1920 px long edge at 16-bit/300dpi. All eight frames must share one preset + one auto-tone + the same exposure/contrast/highlights/temperature/HSL/vibrance offsets, and each of those connector calls is made explicitly on each frame group (no "conceptually applied" hand-waving) - consistency is provable from the step list, not eyeballed. Brand accents must survive the grade - the gold accent must not skew green and the ultramarine must not crush to black. The backdrop must be the licensed Stock asset (provide license proof), and rights must be cleared for unlimited global use.

**Iterative chaining:** The chain is a true output->input spine, not parallel one-shots. The clean bottle capture is uploaded (step 4->5) to a URN consumed by the 16:9 generative expand (step 6), the background-removal cut-out (step 8), and the preset that seeds the grade (step 10). The transparent-bg cut-out from step 8 + the hand plate feed the LOCAL composite (step 9). The graded product base is built by an 8-call chain (preset 10, auto_tone 12, exposure 13, brightness/contrast 14, highlights 15, color_temperature 16, hsl 17, vibrance 18) that finalizes both monreale_graded_product_base and monreale_grade_recipe.json - every recipe key corresponds to a real call. The graded base (step 10) also feeds the 9:16 story expand (step 11). The SAME recorded recipe is then re-invoked on the wide-hero, story and lifestyle composite frame groups (step 19), so consistency is enforced by replaying one recorded chain, not by independently grading each frame. The licensed Stock backdrop (step 3) is fed into both expands (steps 6, 11) so the outpaint blends into real licensed ground. The 8 exact-spec frames are then cut from the graded URNs (step 20 - 3 distinct product crops, 2 hero, 1 story, 2 lifestyle crops), dual-format exported (step 21 = 16 files), and assembled with the inspiration sheet into the Firefly preview board (step 22). Every generative step (the two expands) consumes an earlier generated URN; the composite is the only off-connector [L] step and it too consumes connector-produced cut-out URNs; nothing downstream regenerates from scratch.


**Why it's hard:** Hard for an agent on several axes. (1) Long horizon: 22 dependent connector calls where a mistake early (wrong preset, wrong recipe value) silently corrupts every downstream frame. (2) Heavy state tracking: the agent must read exact specs from the brand-sheet PDF (palette hexes, 3840x2160, 1080x1920, 2048px, 16-bit/300dpi, 8-frame plan) and hold them across the run; it must track which URN is the clean base vs cut-out vs graded base vs each expanded/composited frame. (3) Capability discipline: the agent must NOT reach for image_fill_area as a retoucher or compositor (both A6 [X]) and must instead know that image_generative_expand is the only generative tool, that background-removal + a local composite is the honest path for placement, and that the recorded grade recipe must map 1:1 to real adjustment calls. (4) Asset reuse + consistency: one grade recipe must be provably reused on all 8 frames via an explicit replayed chain, not eyeballed - independent re-grading is the obvious failure mode and is wrong. (5) Exact-spec pressure: the 16:9 and 9:16 outputs must be pixel-exact and dual-format (16 files), with a color-fidelity trap (gold must not green-skew, ultramarine must not crush) that only holds if the same recipe is honestly reused. (6) Frame branching: 3 distinct product-hero crops and 2 lifestyle crops must be produced by the chain. (7) It must also satisfy the soft deliverable - the client's explicit mid-run 'quick preview' Firefly board.

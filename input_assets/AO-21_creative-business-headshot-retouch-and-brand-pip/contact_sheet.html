Full client brief
I'm a founder who needs a creative, dynamic headshot for my professional business profile (LinkedIn + website "About"). My photographer shot me in-studio against a seamless mid-gray backdrop but only gave me the flat, un-retouched RAW-export JPEG plus my simple wordmark monogram as a PNG — I need the actual finished, polished delivery set.

Please, working only from the files I provide:
1. Straighten and clean up the frame, then apply a professional studio tone so the exposure and contrast look intentional, not flat.
2. Warm the skin tone slightly (studio strobes left it a touch cool) and give the whole image a cohesive graded look — I want it to read "premium business portrait," not phone snapshot.
3. Do tasteful, believable retouching: brighten my eyes and teeth a little using selective adjustments (no plastic skin, no face reshaping).
4. Make it feel "dynamic": separate me from the background with a soft blur behind the subject, and reframe the shot into a wider 16:9 environmental crop (using the empty backdrop room on one side) so it works as a hero image with breathing room for text.
5. Give me a tasteful monochrome alternate of the final color portrait for places that want B&W.
6. Produce the profile crops I'll actually upload: a 1:1 LinkedIn/avatar crop and a 1584x396 LinkedIn banner crop, both off the finished color portrait.
7. Vectorize my flat monogram PNG into a clean SVG logomark so it stays crisp on print and large web.
8. Put everything — color hero, mono alt, 1:1, banner, vector logo — into one delivery folder and give me a preview.

Deliverable: a print/web-ready professional headshot package for my business profile.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 20 connector calls, 16 distinct Adobe tools, spanning two capability groups (A1 raster/tonal/selection/FX/crop/vector + A4 asset/storage). It exercises the hardest-to-sequence pattern — mask-driven SELECTIVE edits (select_by_prompt → adjust_light_portions for eyes/teeth; select_subject → invert → gaussian_blur for background-only separation) — which requires correctly threading a mask output into a later edit step rather than applying it globally. It reframes the tall studio master into the 16:9 landscape hero purely by cropping (image_crop_and_resize, no pixels invented), then fans that single master out into four derived deliverables, plus an independent vectorize sub-chain, then storage/folder/preview packaging. No prohibited capabilities: no generative expand/outpaint, no gen-fill, no AI object removal, no text-to-image, no compositing, no bg-replace-by-prompt, no upscaling, no layout composition; fill_area is never used; all edits are real Photoshop/Lightroom-class operations. Every step is [C] headless-confirmed, so it runs end-to-end autonomously, raising the difficulty bar (no human gallery pick to lean on).

Full client brief
Little Lighthouse Fund, a children's charity, is running a fundraising T-shirt campaign and needs its two established concepts turned into production-ready screen-print artwork. The design lead has handed off two flat, high-contrast raster concept mockups exported from their design tool — a front-chest lockup (a stylized lighthouse-and-wave badge with the words LITTLE LIGHTHOUSE) and a full-back design (a larger lighthouse scene with a supporter tagline) — plus a one-page brand-guidelines PDF that pins the campaign palette to exactly three approved spot colors: Beacon Red (PANTONE 186 C, hex #C8102E), Harbor Navy (PANTONE 2758 C, hex #1B2A5B), and Sand Cream (PANTONE 7401 C, hex #F0E6C8). The printer's constraints are strict and non-negotiable: MAXIMUM 3 spot colors per shirt, NO gradients, NO halftones, all lettering already flattened into the raster (no live fonts needed), and each color must sit on its own clean, solid separation so it can be burned to a screen. The as-supplied concept files are muddy: the flat art has slight anti-aliased fringing at the edges, a busy off-white paper-texture background bleeding into the art, and colors that drift a few shades off the approved PANTONE hexes. The job: (1) for BOTH the front and back art, isolate each of the three brand regions by prompt selection and force it to the exact approved spot-color hex as a solid flat fill (no gradient, no tint ramp) so each plate is press-clean; (2) drop the paper-texture background to transparent so only the artwork remains; (3) vectorize each cleaned, single-color layer into crisp scalable vector so the plates hold a hard edge at full front/back print size; (4) deliver one separation vector per color per placement (6 plates total: front R/N/C + back R/N/C), a transparent-background composite vector of each full design, and a single combined proof PDF the printer can check color count and registration against; (5) stage everything into a tidy CC folder and drop a print-shop recipe note listing the PANTONE numbers, placement sizes, and mesh guidance so the printer can run it without a call. Front chest prints ~10cm wide, full back ~30cm wide. Every color stays within the approved 3-spot palette; nothing new is drawn.

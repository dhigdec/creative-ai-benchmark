Full client brief
Loomhearth Supply Co., a men's everyday-basics label refreshing its eBay and marketplace storefront, needs a clean, consistent flat-lay listing pack built from photos already shot in-studio. The owner has handed off six raw overhead flat-lay frames (a heather-gray crewneck tee, a black pullover hoodie, an olive field jacket, a navy waffle henley, a striped rugby, and a folded pair of selvedge jeans), each shot directly overhead but on a mismatched, cluttered styling surface — some on a wooden bench, some on a light linen sheet, all slightly tilted, a touch cool and flat, with stray props and shadow at the edges. Because the listings are photographed piecemeal over several sessions the backgrounds do NOT match, which looks messy across a gallery. The job: (1) straighten every frame so the garment sits square to the crop; (2) true up each garment's color and exposure so fabric reads crisp and true-to-life (correct the cool cast, lift flat exposure, gently push vibrance — subtle post only, no heavy stylizing); (3) lift each garment cleanly off its surface — select the garment as subject, then remove the background so each piece is an isolated transparent PNG with clean edges (no stray props, no surface, no shadow); (4) place every garment onto ONE consistent flat mid-gray backdrop (the owner's preferred neutral that keeps dark and light fabrics balanced on screen) by re-selecting the garment, inverting the selection and solid-filling the background region with an identical mid-gray tone behind all six, so the gallery looks like a matched set; (5) crop and resize every finished item to one uniform square (1:1) marketplace frame at high resolution (3000 px short side) plus a web-ready smaller size. Deliverables: six background-removed transparent PNGs (for reuse), six composited-on-mid-gray high-res JPEGs, six web-ready JPEGs, and a contact-sheet review board so the owner can approve the matched set in one deep-link. True-to-color, consistent, and clean — nothing stylized or invented.

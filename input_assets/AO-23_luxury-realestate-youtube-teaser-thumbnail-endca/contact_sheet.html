Full client brief
A top-producing Manhattan luxury real-estate broker runs a YouTube channel (market insights, property walkthroughs, deal-level expertise) at 3-4 videos/month and needs the full content package built around each video, with luxury-brand restraint and a 48-72h turnaround. For THIS video — a 12-minute Tribeca penthouse walkthrough that opens with a market-insight monologue — deliver three things.

THUMBNAIL: a high-contrast, click-worthy, on-brand thumbnail base plate at 1280x720, NOT a generic headshot with a text box. Start from the broker's supplied studio portrait and the penthouse hero still. Straighten and auto-tone the portrait, isolate the broker from the background so the figure reads cleanly, and grade the penthouse hero into a moody high-contrast luxury look (deepened shadows, controlled highlights, warm prestige temperature, punchy local contrast). Crop/resize the hero to the 16:9 thumbnail frame and, since the wide-angle source still has room to spare, crop the graded hero to the exact 1280x720 thumbnail bounds so it fills the full frame without stretching. Also vectorize the broker's wordmark logo to a clean SVG for crisp placement at any size. The on-thumbnail HEADLINE TEXT is the single step the image connector cannot do (it cannot set type), so it is finished in one local packaging step over the delivered base plate.

TEASER: from the raw 1080p walkthrough footage (which carries the broker's lavalier voice-over), first summarize the footage to locate the single most compelling 60-90 seconds for a podcast-cold-open hook; produce an AI highlight cut to that target duration that hooks in the first 5 seconds and hard-cuts before the resolution; reframe that cut to a 1080x1080 square for LinkedIn cross-posting (same length, no trim); and clean/denoise the broker's voice-over so the spoken track is broadcast-clean. Deliver the YouTube-native teaser and the LinkedIn square, with prestige tone and no cheap transitions.

END CARD: from the client's pre-shot branded end-card backplate footage (10-15s), reframe it to the exact 1920x1080 YouTube delivery dimensions so the subscribe prompt, suggested-video slot and CTA sit correctly and stay consistent across videos.

All image work must be done on the connector (straighten, tone, mask, grade, crop, vectorize); all video/audio work via the async media tools (summarize, quick-cut, resize, speech-clean). Only the final headline typesetting on the thumbnail base plate is local.

Full client brief
I run a silver jewellery line and need my iPhone-captured product frames turned into a polished, store-ready e-commerce set through AI-powered post-production. The look is minimalist and clean: crisp, shadow-controlled pieces on a pure or near-white backdrop, accurate metal tone and true gemstone colour, pin-sharp on every hero angle, with no colour cast — so they drop straight into my online store without further retouching.

Work this one representative hero frame end-to-end as the master recipe, then I will reuse the exact same chain on the rest of the line (the full order is 20 pieces). Start from the original iPhone HEIC/RAW file I supply. First fix the small handheld tilt and set a neutral baseline exposure and white balance, because the iPhone leaves a warm cast that throws off the silver and the gemstone. Then isolate the jewellery itself, drive the background to a uniform pure white with no cast, and grade the piece so the metal highlights read clean-silver and the stone's colour is faithful.

Deliverables for this frame: one finished JPEG at exactly 3000 x 3000 px, sRGB, on uniform white; and one matching PNG of the same piece on a fully transparent background for layering on coloured store sections. Reframe to a true 1:1 square by cropping the existing uniform-white frame to square bounds around the centred piece (do not stretch or distort the piece), then export at the exact pixel spec. Finally, organise the outputs into a tidy delivery folder and assemble the graded pieces into a single review board so I can approve the look before you run the remaining frames.

Acceptance: uniform white background, no colour cast; accurate metal tone and gemstone colour; pin-sharp focus on the hero angle; exact 3000 x 3000 sRGB JPEG plus matching transparent PNG.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon (16 connector calls, all server=adobe, 16 distinct Adobe tools) spanning three groups: asset/storage (init/finalize upload, create_folders, copy_assets, firefly board), raster geometry/tone/colour (auto_straighten, auto_tone, color_temperature, highlights, single_color_saturation), and masked compositing-by-fill (select_by_prompt -> invert -> fill_area -> remove_background -> crop_to_bounds -> crop_and_resize). It is genuinely hard because the honest path must hit a strict pixel/colour acceptance bar (exact 3000x3000 sRGB, no cast, accurate metal+gem) while deliberately AVOIDING the unavailable capabilities the brief naively names: no Generative Fill — the uniform white backdrop is produced by select+invert+solid white fill_area, the 1:1 square is reached by an honest crop of the existing frame (crop_to_bounds then a deterministic crop_and_resize to 3000x3000, no generative canvas extension), and the transparent PNG comes from remove_background, not a prompt-based bg replace. The fork producing two grade-matched deliverables from one master and the select/invert/fill mask interplay reward correct tool semantics; a model that reaches for gen-fill, bg-replace-by-prompt, or stretch-to-square fails the constraints.

Full client brief
Ironclad Elevator & Electrical Supply is a wholesale distributor of electrical and elevator replacement parts selling to service technicians, maintenance supervisors, and electrical contractors. Our buyers open this catalog in the field — often as a phone PDF — when they need to spec a replacement part fast and prepare a quote, so a wrong part number, a blank voltage, or a price that got clipped off the edge of the page is a real problem that costs us an order. We are about to send our new parts catalog to the printer (~100 pages across five sections: Contactors & Relays, Elevator Door Operators, Push-Button Fixtures, Fuses & Breakers, and Cables & Connectors). We already have the InDesign catalog template authored with real data-merge fields (<<part_no>>, <<description>>, <<voltage>>, <<amperage>>, <<list_price>>, <<section>>) laid out as one spec card per part, and our master parts spreadsheet (catalog_parts.csv, ~180 SKUs). Before we spend money on the print run we need a pre-press data-integrity and spec-completeness audit — not a redesign. Specifically: (1) run our authored template through the data merge against the CSV and export a proof PDF so we can see exactly what will print; (2) convert/inspect that proof page by page and read back what actually rendered on each card; (3) extract the printed spec rows and cross-check them against the source CSV to catch part numbers that ran overset (long codes clipped by the frame), any blank/empty voltage-amperage-price cells that would leave a technician guessing, and any section whose printed card count does not match the number of SKUs in the CSV for that section; (4) re-inspect the pages carrying a defect in context and deliver a flagged-pages findings note that keys each defective page number to its issue and part_no so we can find each problem card fast; and (5) deliver a written findings report — a table listing every flagged part number, its page, the field at fault, the CSV value vs. the printed value, and a pass/fail per section — plus a one-line go / no-go recommendation for the print run. Page size is US Letter portrait; keep our section order. Deliver the flagged-pages findings note, the findings report table, the report summary, and the whole audit package as a single zip.

**Template Description: `parts_catalog_template.indd`**

**File Type:** Adobe InDesign Document (.indd)

**Purpose:** This template is designed as a US Letter portrait B2B parts catalog, optimized for data merge to generate individual part specification cards.

**Dimensions:** US Letter (8.5" x 11"), portrait orientation.

**Structure & Layout:**
The template is structured to display multiple "spec cards" per page, with each card detailing a single part. Content is logically grouped under five primary section headers:
1.  Contactors & Relays
2.  Elevator Door Operators
3.  Push-Button Fixtures
4.  Fuses & Breakers
5.  Cables & Connectors

A consistent running header and footer are present across all pages. Each individual part card includes a dedicated, small image frame for a product photograph.

**Layers:**
Standard InDesign layers are employed for organization, typically including:
*   **Text:** For all dynamic and static text elements.
*   **Images:** For product photo frames and any static graphics.
*   **Background/Graphics:** For layout elements, rules, and background colors.
*   **Headers/Footers:** For recurring page elements.

**Placeholders (Data Merge Fields):**
The template is authored with genuine Adobe InDesign data merge fields, ensuring dynamic content population from an external data source. These fields include:
*   `<<part_no>>`
*   `<<description>>`
*   `<<voltage>>`
*   `<<amperage>>`
*   `<<list_price>>`
*   `<<section>>` (used for section grouping/navigation)

**Brand Styling:**
The design follows a clean, professional, and highly functional B2B aesthetic. Emphasis is placed on clear typography, organized presentation of technical specifications, and readability, characteristic of industrial parts catalogs.

**Critical Audit Finding (Intentional Authoring Defect):**
The text frame specifically designated for the `<<part_no>>` data merge field has been deliberately authored with a narrow width. Crucially, auto-fit options (such as "Auto-Fit Frame to Content" or similar) are *not* enabled for this frame. This intentional design flaw will cause text overset and clipping for any alphanumeric part codes that exceed the frame's limited width when data is merged or the document is exported, providing a genuine finding for content overflow during the audit process.

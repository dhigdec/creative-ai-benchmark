Full client brief
I have several old family photo scans that I want to restore and print at A4 to mount on my wall. They suffer from three problems: color fading (the older color shots have gone yellow/orange and flat), scratches or tears (white scratch lines and a torn corner, mostly running across plain backdrops and sky), and blurriness — and the MAIN priority is improving overall clarity so they look crisp on a large print.

Please work from the three supplied scans:
1. faded_color_portrait_1968.png — a colour studio portrait from the late 1960s, heavily yellowed, low contrast, slightly tilted on the scanner bed.
2. scratched_bw_studio_1955.png — a black-and-white studio photo with several fine white scratch lines crossing the plain grey studio backdrop behind the subject.
3. torn_outdoor_snapshot_1972.png — a colour outdoor snapshot with a torn top-left corner exposing white paper and a couple of scratches across the open sky.

For each photo I need you to: straighten any scanner tilt and crop off the ragged scan border; correct the colour cast and faded contrast so skin tones and colours look natural again; lift overall clarity and local contrast so detail reads on a big print; conceal the scratches and the torn corner ONLY where they cross plain, uniform regions (the studio backdrop and the open sky) by colour-matching and filling those marks so they disappear; add a very light film grain so the restored areas don't look plastic against the original photographic texture; and finally produce a clean, true-A4 file at 2480x3508 px / 300 DPI ready to send to the print lab.

Deliverables: three restored, A4 print-ready image files (one per scan), plus a single before/after contact-board so I can approve the restoration at a glance before printing.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 20 connector calls and 14 distinct Adobe tools across 3 groups (asset ingest, the image_* raster family, and the Firefly board), with three independent per-photo restoration sub-chains that converge into a shared grain+A4+board tail — far above the >=10 calls / >=6 distinct tools bar. Difficulty comes from sequencing damage-type-specific fixes correctly (straighten before crop; conceal scratches before re-toning; neutralize cast before recovering saturation; grain last so it overlays the solid-fill patches) and from honest scoping: the agent must recognize that the connector has NO generative heal/clone/object-removal and NO sharpen, so 'scratches/tears' are concealed only where they cross uniform regions via select-by-prompt + solid fill_area, and 'clarity' is delivered through tonal/local-contrast shaping and a clarity preset rather than an invented sharpen op. Every step is [C] headless-confirmed, so the whole workflow is autonomously runnable, but a naive agent that reaches for gen-fill, object-removal, upscaling, or a sharpen tool would violate the capability sheet — making correct tool discipline the core challenge.

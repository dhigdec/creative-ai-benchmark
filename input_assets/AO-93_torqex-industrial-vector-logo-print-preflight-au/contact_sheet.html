Full client brief
Torqex Industrial is a fictional maker of hydraulic torque tools and machinery, and we are about to send our brand mark out to a nameplate vendor to be laser-etched onto anodized-aluminium equipment plates and screen-printed onto tool cases. A previous contractor delivered our 'Torqex' logo as a vector pack, but before it goes to the etching/print house we need a fresh set of eyes to confirm the artwork is genuinely production-ready — the last vendor rejected a batch because a logo file had live text and an RGB blue that shifted on the plate, and we cannot afford another rejected run. This is an AUDIT, not a redesign: do NOT redraw, restyle, re-colour or re-letter anything. You will receive two things. (1) torqex_logo_pack: the delivered artwork — torqex_master.ai (the editable vector master), torqex_master.eps and torqex_master.pdf (the same mark exported), torqex_logo.svg (the web export), plus torqex_onecolour_black.ai (single-colour knockout) and torqex_reverse_white.ai (reverse/knockout-on-dark variant). (2) torqex_nameplate_print_spec.pdf, the vendor's print-spec sheet stating the rules the pack must satisfy: all colours must be CMYK (or spot) — no RGB; the approved brand colour is Torqex Blue at C100 M72 K18 (do NOT ship RGB #1B49E5); all text must be converted to outlines (no live fonts); paths must be closed with no stray/open anchor points; the minimum printable stroke weight for laser etching is 0.25 pt (0.09 mm) — anything thinner drops out; the mark must sit on a transparent background with a 3 mm bleed on the placed variants; the one-colour and reverse variants must be a single flat colour with no gradients or tints. Go through every file, compare what is actually in the artwork against the spec, and hand back a print-readiness preflight report that, for each issue found, gives the file name, the region / element on the mark, a plain-language description of the defect, the specific spec clause it violates, the observed value vs the approved value, and a severity (blocker / warning / nit). We expect you to catch things like: the SVG and the .pdf export carrying RGB colour instead of CMYK; the tagline text in the master left as live (un-outlined) fonts; a couple of stray/duplicate anchor points and one un-closed path on the icon; a hairline register line under 0.25 pt that will drop out on the plate; the reverse variant accidentally carrying a faint gradient instead of a flat white; and one variant exported on a solid white background instead of transparent with no bleed. Some files are clean and compliant so the audit has to distinguish good from bad, not just flag everything.

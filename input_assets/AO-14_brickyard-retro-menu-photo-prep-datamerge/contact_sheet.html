Full client brief
Brickyard, a retro street-food burger joint, needs its one-page menu finished for print. The owner has handed off an authored InDesign menu template (menu_template.indd) laid out in a rustic chalkboard style with four photo frames and real data-merge fields (<<section>>, <<item>>, <<desc>>, <<price>> across Classic Burgers, Specialty Burgers, Sides, Beverages), the brand wordmark as a flat PNG, four raw phone-shot food photos (a double Brickyard, a specialty bacon-jalapeño burger, a basket of fries, a craft cola), and a CSV of placeholder item names / descriptions / prices. The four food photos are unappetizing as-shot: slightly tilted, dull/flat, cool white-balance, busy backgrounds. The job: (1) make every food photo look vibrant and appetizing — give the hero double-smash shot the full grade (straighten, auto-tone, warm the white balance toward a hot street-food look, push vibrance, apply a food-pop preset, and throw the cluttered background out of focus so the burger reads as hero), and bring the other three to a matching appetizing look through their own lighter auto-tone / warm-up (plus a background lens-blur where the background is busy) sub-chains; consistently crop all four to the same frame ratio the template expects; (2) lift the hero double-smash shot cleanly onto a transparent background so it can sit over the chalkboard without a hard photographic edge; (3) vectorize the flat wordmark PNG into a clean SVG so it stays razor-sharp at large print size; (4) license a rustic chalkboard/blackboard texture from Adobe Stock to back the layout; (5) upload all the prepped assets, then run the client's authored .indd through InDesign data-merge against the placeholder-prices CSV so each section/item/price binds into the template, and export the bound document to a print-ready PDF and a high-resolution JPEG. Every section/price stays placeholder per the brief.

Take the client's three raster logo-concept renders and turn them into a clean, scalable brand-asset set: background-removed transparent masters, a color-corrected primary, a one-color (single-ink) version, a circular badge crop, and a vectorized SVG — all assembled on a Firefly board for sign-off.

Full client brief
Maple Ridge Home Services is launching a premium-but-warm home-services brand (tagline: "Clean. Caring. Consistent.") and has early concept artwork it needs elevated into a polished, scalable asset system. The client supplies three raster files: (1) a photographed full-color primary lockup (maple-leaf icon + wordmark) shot on a cream studio sweep, (2) the maple-leaf-only icon photographed on a cluttered wooden workshop desk (busy background that must be stripped), and (3) a circular-badge concept render. The freelancer must NOT redraw the mark — the work is to clean, color-correct, and productize the existing concepts into the exact deliverables the brief lists.\n\nPipeline: ingest the three client files into a working folder (finalizing them into three per-file CC asset URNs); straighten and tightly crop the primary lockup; auto-tone then warm/normalize its color toward the requested warm olive-sage / muted-terracotta / cream palette and lift the cream background to a clean even ivory; isolate the maple-leaf icon from its busy desk shot via prompt-driven subject selection and background removal to a transparent PNG; from that clean transparent icon, derive a one-color (single-ink terracotta) version using a flat color overlay for use on uniforms/yard signs where only one ink is available; build the circular badge by removing the badge render's background and cropping it square-to-round bounds; vectorize the cleaned icon to a scalable SVG for trucks and large-format signage. Deliverables: transparent-PNG master set, a color-graded primary, a one-color version, a circular badge, and an SVG.
Deliverables (5)
  • primary_logo_graded.png — Color-corrected, straightened, tightly cropped primary lockup; warm olive-sage/terracotta/ivory palette, even ivory background; high-res PNG for web headers and invoices.
  • maple_icon_transparent.png — Maple Ridge icon isolated from the busy desk shot, background fully removed, transparent PNG master for flexible placement.
  • maple_icon_onecolor.png — Single-ink (muted terracotta) flat version of the transparent icon for one-color application on uniforms, yard signs and stamps; transparent PNG.
  • circular_badge_transparent.png — Background-removed circular badge, cropped to its round bounds; transparent PNG for social avatars and truck doors.
  • maple_icon.svg — Clean vectorized SVG of the maple-leaf icon for infinite-scale signage and embroidery (the source/scalable deliverable the brief requests alongside AI/EPS).
Workflow — 15 tool steps
  1. asset_create_folders — Create a /Maple Ridge-and-Co working folder in CC storage to hold the three client uploads and all derived variants.
  2. asset_initialize_file_upload — Initialize uploads for the three client raster concepts (primary_lockup_concept.png, maple_icon_on_desk.jpg, circular_badge_concept.png) into the project folder
  3. asset_finalize_file_upload — Finalize the uploads so each client file becomes its own CC asset URN usable by the image_* tools: primary_urn (primary_lockup_concept.png), icon_urn (maple_ico
  4. image_auto_straighten — Straighten the off-angle photographed primary lockup so the wordmark baseline is level.
  5. image_crop_to_bounds — Tightly crop the straightened primary lockup to the artwork, dropping the surplus cream sweep.
  6. image_apply_auto_tone — Auto-tone the cropped primary to fix the dull photographed exposure/contrast as a neutral starting point.
  7. image_adjust_color_temperature — Warm the white balance toward the requested cozy olive-sage/terracotta/ivory palette.
  8. image_select_by_prompt — Select the off-white background area of the warmed primary (prompt: 'cream paper background') to isolate it for cleanup.
  9. image_fill_area — SOLID-fill the selected cream-paper-background region with a clean even ivory RGB swatch, removing the uneven paper blotches -> final graded primary.
  10. image_select_by_prompt — On the busy desk shot, prompt-select the maple-leaf icon card ('maple-leaf logo icon on white card') to separate it from the cluttered wood-desk background.
  11. image_remove_background — Remove the desk background using the subject mask -> transparent-PNG maple-leaf icon master.
  12. image_apply_color_overlay — Flatten the transparent icon to a single muted-terracotta ink via a solid color overlay -> one-color version for uniforms/yard signs.
  13. image_remove_background — Remove the gray-tabletop background from the circular badge concept render -> transparent badge.
  14. image_crop_to_bounds — Crop the transparent badge to its tight round bounds -> square-framed transparent circular badge.
  15. image_vectorize — Vectorize the clean transparent maple-leaf icon into a scalable SVG for large-format signage/embroidery.
Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon (15 connector calls, 12 distinct Adobe tools) spanning two groups — asset/storage and photo/raster — with three parallel sub-chains (primary grade, icon isolation, badge) each terminating in its own saved deliverable. It is honestly scoped to the connector's real ceiling: no mark is drawn from scratch (A6 [X] illustration is avoided), image_fill_area is used only as a solid ivory fill of an inverted selection (not generative/removal), and the SVG comes from image_vectorize rather than a render_vector export of a nonexistent authored .ai. The hard parts are genuine: prompt-isolating the maple-leaf card from a deliberately cluttered wood-desk photo, deriving a one-color ink version via flat overlay, and producing transparent + vector masters — exactly the 'bg_removal_batch' work this candidate is categorized as. Every step is [C] headless-confirmed, so the whole chain is autonomously runnable with no [W]/[A]/[T] dependency.

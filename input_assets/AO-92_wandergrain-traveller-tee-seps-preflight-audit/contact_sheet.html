Full client brief
Wandergrain Supply Co., a small-batch apparel screen-print shop, has just received four traveller-themed t-shirt graphics from a freelance designer for its new "Modern Wanderer" tee line (universal travel icons -- a passport-stamp badge, a latitude/longitude coordinate mark, a minimalist skyline, and a compass-rose crest). Before any of these hit the exposure room and burn screens, the shop needs an independent pre-flight / separation audit -- the designer swears the files are "pre-flighted for screen print," but the shop has been burned before by art that looked fine on screen and fell apart on the press. We are NOT redesigning anything and NOT producing final films; we need an honest QA report. For each of the four designs, please verify and document: (1) background integrity -- the art must sit on a genuinely transparent background with no stray off-white halo or leftover matte that would print as a phantom screen; (2) spot-color / ink count -- count the distinct flat spot colors in each design and flag any design that exceeds our 4-color manual-press limit, or that hides near-duplicate colors that should be merged to one screen; (3) registration & trap readiness -- check whether the art carries registration marks / crop guides and whether abutting colors are cleanly separable or will need a choke/trap note; (4) detail survivability -- flag any hairline strokes, tiny text, or fine detail below what a 156-mesh screen can hold, and confirm the "keep text editable" promise by checking whether type is still crisp vector or has been flattened to messy raster; (5) vector cleanliness -- confirm each design is truly vector (not a raster PNG masquerading as art) by test-vectorizing and comparing. Deliverables: a per-design marked-up preview of each graphic (so we can circle problems), plus a single written seps pre-flight report (a recipe/findings note) that lists, per design, the spot-color count, pass/fail on transparency, registration presence, minimum-detail concern, and a clear GO / FIX-FIRST / NO-GO verdict the exposure room can act on. Files supplied as an Illustrator-exported package: four graphics (three already flattened to PNG with transparency, one still a layered vector) plus the designer's own color-spec sheet. Keep everything spot-color / vector oriented -- no CMYK conversions, no re-coloring, no new artwork.

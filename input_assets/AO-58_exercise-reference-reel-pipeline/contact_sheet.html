Full client brief
We run a personal-training business with a library of 5,000+ exercises and want to turn raw footage into short, consistent educational reference videos for coach education and client support — today each one takes 20-30 min in Premiere and we want a repeatable, AI-assisted production pipeline. This task is ONE run of that pipeline on a single representative exercise, the Goblet Squat, so we can validate the repeatable unit before scaling it across the library.

Supplied assets: (1) a raw ~45s 4K landscape clip of a coach demoing the goblet squat with spoken form cues recorded in a slightly echoey gym (camera mic); (2) our gym's square logo PNG (dark logo on transparent-ish background); (3) a flat hand-drawn red 'muscle-activation' arrow/callout PNG we use to point at the working muscle groups.

What we need produced this run:
1. SUBTITLE & CUE SOURCE — an AI content summary / transcript-style scene summary of the raw clip we can paste into Premiere as the subtitle + on-screen coaching-cue source text, and which we'll also use to name the clip consistently.
2. CLEAN VOICEOVER — the coach's spoken cues de-echoed / denoised into a clean voice track to drop back under the reel (voice only — leave the music/ambience decision to us).
3. REUSABLE WATERMARK — our logo turned into a transparent, corner-sized, white version that reads on dark gym footage (this becomes a reusable overlay asset for the whole library).
4. REUSABLE MUSCLE-GROUP CALLOUT — the hand-drawn arrow cleaned up into a crisp scalable vector recolored to our brand accent, sized as an overlay element (also reusable library-wide).
5. VERTICAL REEL — the raw clip reframed to 1080x1920 vertical (full length, no trimming at this step) and then cut down by AI into a punchy ~30s highlight 'reference reel' of the key reps.
6. ORGANIZED LIBRARY ENTRY — a folder following our naming convention (Exercise / LowerBody / GobletSquat) with the finished reel + the reusable overlay assets copied in, ready to template across the next 5,000 clips.

Honesty note for the vendor: we understand the actual frame-by-frame compositing (burning subtitles in, placing the watermark and arrow onto the video, transitions) stays in Premiere on our side — what we want from this automated pipeline is every input asset and the reframed/cut reel prepared and organized so that final Premiere step is fast and identical every time.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 15 connector calls, 14 distinct Adobe tools spanning four tool groups (asset/storage, media video+audio [A], raster image [C], and the vectorize path), with three parallel sub-chains (media, watermark, callout) that converge into one organized library deliverable. It exercises real cross-group dependency tracking (the raw_clip_asset fan-out, the 4-input final copy) and forces correct execution-mode handling — the [A] async video/audio steps interleave with [C] headless image steps. It is honestly bounded: it deliberately stops at asset preparation + reframe + AI cut + organization and explicitly leaves frame-by-frame burn-in/compositing/transitions to the human Premiere step, since Adobe-connector video compositing, subtitle burn-in, and overlay placement are not available — making the difficulty come from orchestration and faithful capability mapping rather than from any disallowed generative/compositing shortcut.

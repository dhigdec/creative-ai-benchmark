AO-48 — Convert an AI-Generated Logo into a Clean, Multi-Platform Vector Kit

Technology, SaaS & Startups ready for agent brand-kit consistent

Take the client's AI-generated raster logo, clean and de-skew it, isolate the mark on transparency, trace it into a crisp scalable SVG, and derive a full multi-platform vector + raster export kit that stays high-quality at every size and format.

💰 ~$95 est.Upwork20 tool calls17 distinct toolslong-horizon
Full client brief
Northwind Analytics generated their logo in AI and only has a flat 1024x1024 PNG of it — a teal-and-charcoal wordmark-plus-icon (a stylized wind/data-flow glyph) sitting on a faint off-white textured card, with the soft anti-aliased edges, slight noise, and a baseline that tips a couple of degrees off level that AI image tools typically produce. They cannot use this raster on their website header, app icon, pitch deck, sign vinyl, or one-color merch because it does not scale and has no transparency. The freelancer must deliver a production vector kit: (1) auto-straighten and tonally clean the raster so edges are sharp and the background is even; (2) tighten the crop to the artwork; (3) isolate just the logo mark from its background card and flatten the background to pure white so the trace is clean; (4) produce a transparent cut-out of the mark; (5) VECTORIZE the cleaned mark into a crisp full-color SVG that scales to any size; (6) derive a single-color / knockout vector (logo silhouette filled solid for one-color print, vinyl, embroidery and small favicons) by selecting the mark, inverting, solid-filling the field, and re-vectorizing; (7) produce a halftone "print-screen" treatment of the mark for merch/poster use; (8) recommend a brand-safe font pairing that matches the wordmark so the client can extend the identity in decks and docs; and (9) export platform-sized PNG rasters (a 1024 app icon, a web header bug, a 512 social avatar) from the cleaned mark and assemble all generated deliverables into a single shareable presentation board with an organized asset folder. Everything must remain high-quality across formats — vectors stay editable/scalable, rasters are crisp at their target sizes.

Workflow — 20 tool steps

  3. image_auto_straighten — Correct the ~2.5deg tilt baked into the AI export so the wordmark baseline is level — essential before any clean trace.
  4. image_adjust_brightness_and_contrast — Boost contrast and even out the mild AI lighting so the teal/charcoal artwork separates cleanly from the off-white card — sharper edges for the vectorizer.
  5. image_crop_to_bounds — Trim the surrounding card so the canvas hugs the artwork, removing wasted background area before isolating the mark.
  6. image_select_by_prompt — Select just the logo mark (the teal/charcoal icon + wordmark) via prompt, producing a maskURI separating artwork from the textured background.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 20 connector calls, 17 distinct Adobe tools, spanning 3 groups (A1 photo/raster, A2 document/font, A4 asset/board). It is genuinely hard because the order matters — you must straighten, contrast-clean, crop, isolate via select->invert->solid-fill->remove-background, and only THEN vectorize, or the trace inherits the AI noise/tilt; a naive agent that vectorizes the raw PNG fails the 'high-quality across formats' bar. It also tests honest tool boundaries: image_fill_area is used correctly as a solid-color flatten (not generative), the platform PNGs are honest crop/resize reframes to their target ratios (not canvas extensions), the from-scratch vector comes only from image_vectorize (not document_render_vector, which can't author a vector), and the one-color variant is correctly built via color_overlay->re-vectorize rather than any unavailable gen step. The branch-and-converge topology (one cleaned mark fanning into seven deliverables, then collected into a folder and a board) exercises multi-output reuse end to end.

Edit the client's three supplied room frames (living room, kitchen, bedroom) into clean, modern, naturally-lit real-estate listing photos with straight verticals and corrected color, then export each in web-optimized JPEG (<2 MB) and full-resolution TIFF for Airbnb/Zillow.

Full client brief
A landlord listing a freshly renovated rental on Airbnb and Zillow has captured interior frames of the three key areas — living room, kitchen, and bedroom — during a 0700-1200 natural-light window, but the RAW-style frames are tilted, slightly under-exposed in the shadows, mixed-white-balance (warm tungsten vs cool window daylight), and carry minor wall scuffs and dust marks. They want the post-production handled to a "modern, clean, simple, ample lighting, natural" standard: straight verticals and distortion-free perspective, basic color correction, gentle shadow lift and highlight recovery so the rooms read bright and spacious, quick blemish spotting on wall scuffs/dust WITHOUT an over-processed look, and final files delivered in two formats per the brief — web-optimized JPEG under 2 MB each AND full-resolution TIFF — within three days.

The freelancer must run an Adobe photo-editing chain per room: license/load the supplied frame, auto-straighten the verticals, crop out the resulting wedge to a clean distortion-free frame, apply auto-tone as a neutral baseline, neutralize the mixed white balance with a color-temperature correction, lift the dark portions and recover the blown window highlights, selectively brighten the room interior via a prompt-based mask so the space feels open without flattening the windows, spot-correct wall scuffs/dust by selecting the blemish and filling it with the matched neutral wall color (solid fill only — not generative), apply a final gentle vibrance/saturation lift for the "modern clean" feel, then crop-and-resize to the two delivery specs. Each room's edit is then assembled onto a Firefly Board so the client can review all three corrected listing shots in one deep-link before download. Across the three rooms the chain reuses the straighten→crop→tone→temperature→shadow/highlight→selective-brighten→spot-fix→vibrance→resize pattern, every step consuming the previous step's output.

Why it's non-trivial

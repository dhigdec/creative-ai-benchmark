Full client brief
Rukmini Silverworks, a boutique 925 sterling-silver house, is re-listing its collection across its own store and two marketplaces and needs clean, consistent product imagery so the metalwork stays the hero. The owner has handed off six raw tabletop frames shot on a phone under mixed studio light — a filigree pendant, a pair of jhumka drop earrings, a stacking band ring, a chain bracelet, an oxidised cuff, and a solitaire-look ring. As-shot they are inconsistent: slightly tilted, framed loose with a grey sweep and prop clutter visible, dull/flat exposure, and a warm-to-cool colour cast that makes the silver look muddy or yellow rather than bright neutral. The job is a batch clean-up, not a re-shoot: for every SKU (1) straighten and level the piece; (2) neutralise the white balance and lift the exposure so the silver reads as bright, cool-neutral metal with no colour cast; (3) gently push clarity/contrast and, on the oxidised piece, hold the dark detail so the antique recesses stay legible; (4) select the jewellery as the subject, invert that selection to a background-only mask, and solid-fill it with pure #FFFFFF so each piece sits on a flat, shadow-free white backdrop (the metalwork must keep its true edge — no halo, no chopped prongs, open chain-links and prong gaps preserved); (5) crop every SKU to an identical centered square and export at 3000 x 3000 px, sRGB, web-ready; (6) from the same graded master, strip the background to a clean transparent PNG for the store's own layered layouts. Deliverables per SKU: a transparent-background PNG and a 3000 x 3000 white-#FFFFFF JPEG, all six looking like a matched set. Brand constraint: pure #FFFFFF background (hex exact), no visible shadow, no colour cast, metal is the hero — the pieces must look like one consistent catalog, not six different edits.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon batch flagship: 45 connector calls, 14 distinct Adobe tools, far above the >=10 calls / >=6 distinct-tool floor. Difficulty comes from running six separate SKUs through an identical multi-step isolate-on-white pipeline while handling per-image variance (warm cast on some, cool on others; an oxidised piece needing a dark-portions lift so detail survives; a chain whose link-gaps must land in the background mask and not be painted as metal; a reflective solitaire whose specular edge must be retained), each SKU forking one graded master into a select->invert->solid-fill white-BG JPEG and a remove_background transparent PNG, then converging twelve deliverables into folder storage and an inline-preview QA pass. It respects every hard limit: the white background is produced non-generatively by image_select_subject -> image_invert_selection -> image_fill_area (SOLID #FFFFFF fill of the background mask), exactly the reference pattern, NOT by feeding a transparent PNG into fill; image_remove_background is used only to lift a graded master to a transparent PNG; NO generative fill / expand / reframe-crop, NO upscaling / super-resolution, NO inpainting-of-occluded-pixels, NO text-to-image / from-scratch art, NO bespoke fonts, NO background-replacement-by-prompt. The pieces are inspected via asset_inline_preview, not regenerated.

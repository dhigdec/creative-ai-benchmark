Full client brief
We are launching a new corporate website for our industrial / CNC-machining business and need a Photoshop expert to professionally retouch a set of images so they look premium, modern, and consistent. Attached are two client-shot RAW-ish JPEGs straight off the camera: (1) HERO — a process engineer standing beside a CNC machining centre on the plant floor, shot under mixed fluorescent + window light so it is slightly underexposed, cool/green-cast, and the background is cluttered with parts bins, cables and a fire extinguisher; (2) a paired executive half-body portrait shot in the same facility, warmer but flat and low-contrast.\n\nWe need you to: correct the lighting imbalance and exposure on the hero; pull the white balance neutral and add real contrast, color and sharpness so it reads premium; clean up the distracting background clutter behind the engineer (we don't want a fake studio look — keep it authentic, just calm it down) by selecting the subject, inverting to a background-only region and laying a neutral fill over the clutter, then give the whole frame a gentle lens-blur soft-focus so it feels like a shallow-depth premium shot (the subject stays clean because the clutter around it was filled, not erased with AI); then carry the EXACT same finished look onto the executive portrait so the two images are visually consistent as a set. Once the look is locked, prepare web-optimized, responsive exports of the hero at desktop (1920px wide), tablet (1024px) and mobile (640px) sizes, all centred on the subject and fast-loading. Finally, collect the graded hero, the matching portrait and the three responsive crops into a single review board so our marketing lead can approve the style in one click before we roll it across the rest of the site library. Keep everything natural and realistic — this is a real factory, not a render.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 20 steps, 14 distinct Adobe tools (well above the ≥10 calls / ≥6 distinct-tool floor), spanning asset I/O, a multi-stage tonal/color grade, mask-based selective editing, preset-driven cross-image consistency, responsive multi-size export, and board assembly. Difficulty comes from (a) the multi-branch dependency graph — a linear grade, a select→invert→fill→blur declutter branch reusing an intermediate, and a preset that must be captured once and reused on a second, differently-toned image; (b) capability discipline: the brief literally says 'remove unwanted objects', which a naive agent would attempt via forbidden AI object-removal — the honest solution routes it through select_subject→invert→solid fill_area→lens_blur, the only allowed path; (c) consistency is a genuine test because the portrait starts warm/flat while the hero starts cool/underexposed, so a shared preset must actually unify two different starting points; (d) the 1920/1024/640 responsive fan-out and final convergence into a single board + zip.

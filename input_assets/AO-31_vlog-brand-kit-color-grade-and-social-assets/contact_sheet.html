Full client brief
You are the recurring post-production designer for "Princerrr," a daily-life vlog shot in 4K and delivered at 1080p. The video edit itself (timeline trimming, jump-cuts, the full NLE project file) is handled elsewhere — your contract is the per-episode STILL BRAND KIT and the one media touch below, delivered consistently so every episode looks like the same channel.

Assets the client uploads for this episode: (1) a 4K frame-grab pulled from the hero clip (the creator at their desk, window light), (2) a separate DSLR "shot" still of the creator used for the thumbnail, (3) a flat PNG of the channel wordmark / logo lockup, and (4) the hero clip itself (a 12s 4K horizontal segment).

What to do, as one chained job:
1. Normalize and color-grade BOTH stills to a single house look. Start from the frame-grab: auto-tone it, warm the white balance to match the creator's window-lit "personal, upbeat" feel, lift the shadows and tame the highlights, then push vibrance so it reads lively without going garish, and finally stamp a chosen Lightroom preset so the grade is repeatable across future episodes. Apply the SAME finishing preset to the DSLR shot still so the two match.
2. From the graded DSLR still, build the YouTube thumbnail: isolate the creator (subject), invert to the background, knock the background's saturation down so the subject pops, then crop/resize to a clean 1280×720 16:9 thumbnail.
3. From the subject-popped full-res DSLR still, produce a 9:16 vertical "story / Shorts cover" still by subject-aware reframing/cropping the existing pixels to the 9:16 crop (no re-shoot, no invented canvas) and exporting at 1080×1920.
4. Turn the flat wordmark PNG into a crisp, infinitely-scalable SVG so it can sit in lower-thirds and the end screen without softening; keep that SVG as the channel mark.
5. Media touch: reframe the supplied 12s horizontal hero clip to a 9:16 vertical of the SAME length for Shorts (no trimming).

Deliverables: graded frame-grab + graded DSLR still (matched look), a 1280×720 thumbnail, a 1080×1920 story still, the channel wordmark as SVG, and a 9:16 same-length reframed clip.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 16 connector calls, 13 distinct Adobe tools, spanning three tool groups (asset I/O, the photo/raster image_* family, and the async video media tool). The grade is a genuine multi-stage Lightroom-class chain (auto-tone -> WB -> shadows -> highlights -> vibrance -> preset) rather than a one-shot filter, and the thumbnail requires correct masking logic (select_subject -> invert -> masked saturation) plus a format-bridge crop. It forces the agent to respect real connector limits: it cannot trim or re-cut the video, cannot author an NLE project, and must recognize that the brief's literal video-edit core is out of scope while still delivering the legitimate Adobe-owned slice (color correction + still brand kit + same-length reframe). The one [A] async step means part of the deliverable returns via the product's progress widget, not headlessly — the agent must tag and sequence it correctly. Grounding is faithful (color correction, lower-third/end-screen graphics, thumbnails, 1080p/9:16 social, ongoing per-episode cadence all come straight from the client), while the precise grade parameters and the vectorize/reframe choices are reasonable professional enrichment.

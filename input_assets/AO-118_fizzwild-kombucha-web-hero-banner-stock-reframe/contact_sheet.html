Full client brief
Fizzwild Kombucha, a fictional small-batch craft kombucha brand relaunching its website, needs a cohesive set of website hero and banner graphics built entirely from licensed stock — no from-scratch illustration, no canvas expansion. The client has handed off a brand kit: a flat wordmark PNG (fizzwild_wordmark.png), a hex palette (brand teal #0E8C7F, sunburst amber #F5A623, off-white cream #FAF3E0), and a short text list of the section headers and promo copy that the dev team will typeset live (the freelancer lays in imagery + prepares the reusable wordmark, not body copy). The ask, quoting the brief, is "matching website graphics—hero images, section headers, and call-to-action spots—plus occasional banner ads for promotional campaigns," and every asset "should feel part of one cohesive family while being perfectly sized, compressed, and formatted for its destination." Deliverables and exact dimensions: (1) a desktop homepage hero at 1920x1080; (2) a mobile homepage hero at 1080x1920 built from a SECOND licensed shot (portrait crop) so the phone view is not just a squeezed desktop image; (3) an "Our Flavors" section-header strip at 1600x600; (4) a call-to-action card at 1080x1350; (5) a promotional banner at 1456x180 (leaderboard-style). Brand constraints: the whole set must read as ONE vibrant, warm, energetic family — push the color the same way across every image, warm the white balance slightly, and keep the amber/teal palette feeling present; the wordmark must stay razor-sharp wherever the dev team later places it, so vectorize it and also deliver a transparent cutout PNG. Hard sourcing rule (per the brief and the web context): because these are website heroes, each stock photo MUST be licensed at a resolution LARGER than its destination slot and then cropped/reframed DOWN to fit — never scaled up or canvas-expanded. Required content: bright bottles-of-kombucha / pouring-fizz / fresh-fruit-and-glass lifestyle imagery; a consistent warm vibrant grade across all five; all five photographic slots stay clean of baked-in text and logo so the dev team can overlay the live headline, CTA button, and wordmark on top (matching how a real web hand-off keeps text as live layers). Final handoff: a web-ready pack (correctly-sized JPEGs for the five slots, the wordmark SVG, a transparent wordmark PNG) plus a one-page style/recipe note listing the palette and the exact grade recipe so future assets stay consistent, all zipped, and a Firefly board deep-link so the founder can approve the look in one click.

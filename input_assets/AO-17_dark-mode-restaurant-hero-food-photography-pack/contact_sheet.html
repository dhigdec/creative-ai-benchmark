Full client brief
We are a Portugal-based boutique digital agency building a premium restaurant website. The design is high-end, clean, modern-minimalist, Dark Mode aesthetic, and focuses heavily on high-quality food photography across the Home and Interactive Food Menu sections, fully optimized for mobile. We do NOT need the site built here — our Wix Studio designer handles layout. We need a food-photo retoucher to turn the restaurant's raw plate shots into a polished, cohesive dark-mode hero-image pack.

Supplied raw assets: (1) a hero shot of a signature seared-scallop dish on a dark slate plate, shot slightly tilted under warm mixed restaurant lighting; (2) a flat-lay of a chocolate dessert on a marble board; (3) a close-up of a cocktail with garnish; plus the restaurant's existing flat wordmark logo as a PNG.

Deliverables:
1. A polished, perfectly level dark-mode HERO image — straightened, auto-toned, exposure/contrast set for a moody dark background, the plate subject made to "pop" off the dark surround, cooler-warmer balance dialed to a premium look, then reframed to the 16:9 hero size by cropping the plate off-center to the right so the dish's own near-black charcoal surround gives the web designer clean dark negative space on the left for an overlaid headline (edges cropped to hit the ratio). Export-ready full-bleed desktop hero.
2. Two square MENU-TILE images (dessert + cocktail) graded to match the hero's dark, moody, consistent look so the Interactive Food Menu feels cohesive.
3. One full-bleed 9:16 MOBILE crop of the hero, subject-aware, for the mobile Home section.
4. The flat wordmark logo cleaned to a transparent PNG and vectorized to crisp SVG for retina/responsive use on the dark site.
5. A Firefly Boards moodboard collecting the final hero, menu tiles, and logo as a single deep-link the Wix designer can reference for the dark-mode visual direction.

The whole set must read as one premium, dark, cohesive system. Match grade across all food images. Keep skin/food tones appetizing — dark mode, not muddy.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon flagship: 22 connector calls, 15 distinct Adobe tools, spanning 2 capability groups (A1 raster, A4 asset/board). It is a genuine food_bev_photo retouch job — the hardest, most realistic part of the long-tail of the actual brief. Difficulty comes from (a) a 10-step single-image hero pipeline including a masked selective edit (select_subject to vibrance on the mask) and a two-stage gen-free reframe (crop 3:2 to 16:9 with the subject offset so the existing dark surround becomes left headline negative space, then resize to the export dims), (b) grade-MATCHING across three differently-lit raw captures so the menu reads cohesively (the core 'cohesive dark-mode system' requirement), and (c) a converging dependency graph where four sub-chains must all complete before the moodboard step. Every step is headless-confirmed [C]: no [W] Express widget, no [A] async video/audio, no [T] authored template, no Canva, no local step. No not-available capability is used — no generative-expand/outpaint, no image_fill_area misuse, no compositing/gen-fill/object-removal/upscale/text-to-image, and no layout composition (no render_layout/render_vector as authoring). The web build itself is correctly out of scope and excluded.

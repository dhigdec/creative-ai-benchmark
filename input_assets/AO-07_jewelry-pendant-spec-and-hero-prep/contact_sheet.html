Full client brief
From the industrial designer's raster pendant sketch and two mixed-lighting workbench photos of a stainless-steel sample pendant and chain, produce a clean factory-ready vector spec asset plus retouched, isolated, color-accurate catalog hero shots (one reframed and resized to a roomy catalog hero canvas, one duotone print-screen variant), delivered as a coordinated set for the design team's refinements-and-iterations review.
Deliverables (4)
  • pendant_spec_lineart.svg — Clean scalable SVG of the deskewed, tight-cropped pendant line-art, crisp at any print size, suitable as a dimensional spec reference for the factory.
  • pendant_hero_catalog.png — Retouched, white-balanced, auto-toned sample pendant cut out from the bench onto a clean solid catalog background and reframe-cropped/expanded to a roomy catalog hero canvas (e.g. ~4:5 or wider). PNG.
  • pendant_spec_screenprint.png — High-contrast duotone/halftone 'print-screen' variant of the hero pendant for the black-and-white spec sheet. PNG.
  • chain_hero_catalog.png — The box-link chain color-matched to the pendant (same neutral white balance + tonal treatment) and isolated on the same clean catalog background, so pendant and chain read as a coordinated set. PNG.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 23 connector calls, 14 distinct Adobe tools, three asset-load branches that must be processed independently to produce a coordinated catalog set. It exercises geometry (auto_straighten, crop_to_bounds), vector conversion (vectorize), a full tonal/color grade pipeline (color_temperature, auto_tone, highlights, single_color_saturation), masking/isolation (select_by_prompt -> remove_background -> fill_area), the sole reframe/crop step (generative-expand reframe-crop), and a print FX (halftone). The agent must (a) recognize the brief's literal 3D-CAD core is out of scope and faithfully take the Adobe-doable 2D asset-prep slice, (b) keep the pendant and chain color treatments consistent so the set matches, (c) order masking after grading so the cutout carries the corrected color, and (d) respect tool limits — fill_area is solid-color only (no compositing), generative-expand is the sole reframe/crop step, vectorize needs a clean deskewed crop first.

Full client brief
I run a hands-off Shopify store selling thermal cameras, night-vision optics, firearms, ammunition, trail-camera mounts and hunting accessories. My distributor feeds dump product photos that are all over the place: shot on off-white or gray seamless, slightly tilted, warm/yellow color cast, and framed inconsistently — they look unprofessional next to each other on a collection page and several get rejected by marketplace channels that require a pure-white background. As part of the catalog cleanup I need a repeatable Adobe pipeline that takes one raw distributor photo and produces marketplace-ready listing assets, demonstrated end-to-end on my hero SKU (a handheld thermal weapon scope) and ready to repeat across the catalog.

For the hero SKU, starting from the single raw distributor JPEG I provide, deliver: (1) a corrected master — auto-straightened, cropped to the product, exposure/tone normalized, and the warm color cast neutralized; (2) a pure #FFFFFF background version where the scope is isolated and the messy seamless is replaced with solid white per Shopify/Amazon hero spec; (3) a transparent-PNG cut-out of the same scope for collection tiles and bundle mock-ups; (4) a square 1:1 hero produced by cropping the white-background hero to a centered 1:1 square (the source frame is landscape, so the square is a crop of the existing image — no canvas is invented or extended) and then resized/exported at exactly 2048×2048. Also vectorize the small distributor brand badge I supply (a flat single-color logo mark) into a clean SVG so it scales on the listing, recommend a clean sans-serif catalog typeface, and assemble the finished hero set into a Firefly review board so I can approve it in one place. Finally, drop everything into a tidy, clearly named asset folder I can hand to the next batch.

Constraints: backgrounds must be solid pure white (no gradient, no AI-invented scene), the product must not be distorted or recolored beyond honest color-cast correction, and nothing may be composited in or generatively painted onto the product itself. Keep my color/tone edits faithful to the real product (a firearm optic — accuracy matters to buyers).

No Canva, no Express [W] widget track, no [X] not-available capability (no AI object removal, no compositing, no upscaling, no text-to-image), and no local step — the entire deliverable is achievable Adobe-only and headless.

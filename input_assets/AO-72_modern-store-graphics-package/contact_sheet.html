Full client brief
I'm refreshing my online shop and need a cohesive set of graphics that instantly tells shoppers they're in a sleek, modern space. Scope is three areas. (1) STORE BANNER — a hero that sets the tone the moment visitors land, sized for both desktop (1920x720) and mobile (1080x1350). I'll give you one clean product hero shot to build it from, framed with generous empty space so you can reframe it to both ratios by cropping (no invented pixels). (2) PDP ASSETS — template-ready image frames plus iconography and typography treatments that make features, specs and CTAs pop without clutter. (3) STORE DECORATION — background elements, section dividers and accent graphics that tie everything together page to page. Art direction: clean lines, bold but balanced color, plenty of negative space — nothing fussy or ornamental. I'm supplying brand colors (deep ink #14233B + warm sand #E7D3B1 accent), the product hero shot, a few supporting product crops, and two layered source templates my team authored in InDesign and Illustrator (the banner layout .indd with our logo + headline frames, and an .ai sheet of our icon/divider library) so you can produce the final layered source files and labeled exports we can tweak later. Please also pull one modern, negative-space lifestyle backdrop from stock for the secondary banner panel. Deliverables: layered PSD/AI/INDD source plus optimized JPG/PNG exports for immediate upload, clearly labeled, with desktop and mobile banner sizes, the PDP frame set, the vector icon + divider pack, and the decoration backgrounds. Lock the direction first, then banner, then PDP, then decoration.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 24 steps, 18 distinct Adobe tools spanning four groups (asset/stock, raster photo, vector/document export, organization) plus one allowed local zip. It exercises a realistic agency pipeline — establish a master grade on the hero, propagate it to a stock asset and PDP macros for visual cohesion, fork the same graded hero into two honest crop-only reframes (desktop and mobile ratios), export two genuinely client-authored InDesign/Illustrator sources, vectorize an accent, and build a grain-textured solid decoration background — all chained. Difficulty comes from sequencing dependencies (license-before-edit, straighten/tone before cropping, grade before propagate), reusing inputs across branches, and staying strictly inside real capabilities: the reframe steps are image_crop_and_resize crops of the existing hero (edges cropped away to hit each ratio, no invented pixels), fill_area is solid-color only, and both authored templates are EXPORTED not composed. No not-available capability (generative expand, gen-fill, object removal, text-to-image, compositing, layout composition, upscaling) is used.

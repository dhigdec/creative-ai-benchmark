Full client brief
Maison Valcere is a fictional independent high-jewelry house (fine rings, pendants and bespoke pieces) that has just received its finished logo pack from an outside brand studio and, before it goes onto embossed presentation boxes, foil-stamped certificates, the e-commerce header and the video title cards, wants an independent set of eyes to confirm the mark is genuinely production-clean. The identity is meant to feel unmistakably premium — a deep-black canvas with crisp white typography, minimal ornamentation, purely typographic with no symbols or icons — and the house is strict that every asset stays consistent across packaging, print and screen. You are handed four things: (1) valcere_wordmark_master.svg, the "production" vector master the studio delivered; (2) valcere_wordmark_web.png, the flat raster export the house has been using online; (3) valcere_brand_guideline.pdf, the guideline stating the monochrome palette (Valcere Noir #0E0E10, Champagne #E8DCC2, Blanc #FAF8F3 — with the rule that the wordmark is "purely typographic, no symbols or icons, never recoloured outside the monochrome palette, minimum print width 22mm, minimum hairline stroke 0.35pt / 1.5px at favicon size"); and (4) valcere_press_spec.pdf, the printer/foil spec sheet (foil-stamp minimum stroke, CMYK/spot conversion requirement, "all strokes must be expanded to filled paths, no open contours, no RGB-only fills"). The job is an AUDIT, not a redraw — do NOT re-letter, re-trace, recolour or fix anything, and do NOT deliver a new logo. Go through the mark region by region and compare what the pack actually contains against the guideline and the press spec, then hand back a vector & print-readiness audit report that, for each issue, gives the mark region (which glyph / stroke / variant), a plain-language description of the defect, the specific guideline or press-spec clause it violates, the approved value vs. the observed value, and a severity (blocker / warning / nit). We expect you to catch things like: a hairline serif or connecting stroke thinner than the 0.35pt foil-stamp minimum that will drop out when stamped or shrunk to a favicon; a stray decorative anchor / tiny symbol that violates the "purely typography, no symbols" rule; the reverse (knockout) variant sitting on an off-palette near-black instead of Valcere Noir #0E0E10; kerning drift where the raster web export does not match the vector master's letter-spacing; and paths in the master SVG that are not print-ready (unexpanded strokes, open contours, RGB fills that were never converted for spot/foil). Deliver the report as a clean A4 portrait PDF plus the raw findings as a structured file, and include a one-page cover summary with the total count by severity and a go / no-go-for-press verdict.

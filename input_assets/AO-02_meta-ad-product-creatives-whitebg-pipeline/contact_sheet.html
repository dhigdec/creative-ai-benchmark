Full client brief
DONE is a DTC homeware brand launching paid-social tests for its matte-ceramic pour-over coffee dripper and needs a freelancer to build the imagery layer of a Meta (Facebook + Instagram) ad-creative kit, faithful to the brand guidelines (brand teal #0F6E64 on warm off-white #F4EFE7). You are supplied three client assets: (1) a raw studio product photo of the dripper on a near-white sweep that still shows seamless-paper shadows and a faint gray cast; (2) the DONE wordmark logo as a flat PNG; and (3) the brand-guideline sheet as a PDF for reference. 

Build the production chain entirely on Adobe connector tools: ingest the assets into a working folder; auto-correct the studio shot's geometry and tone; isolate the product and force a true, pure-white e-commerce background (catalog-clean, no gray cast, no stray shadow halo); push a brand-consistent color grade so the ceramic reads warm and premium and the teal accents stay on-palette; then generate the platform frames performance marketers actually test — a 1:1 feed square (1080x1080), a 4:5 portrait feed (1080x1350), and a 9:16 Stories/Reels vertical (1080x1920) — by honestly reframing/cropping the graded hero to each target ratio, keeping the product centered and accepting that the studio edges/background trim away to hit the ratio (no canvas is added, padded, or extended). Separately, vectorize the supplied wordmark to a clean SVG so it stays crisp at any ad size. Finally, assemble the full variation set into a Firefly board as a single review deep-link the client can sign off on in Slack. 

Out of scope for this connector pass (hand off to the typesetting/Express step): baking headline/offer/CTA copy and the logo lockup onto the frames, and choosing final on-image type — though you should return a font recommendation to steer that. Deliverables: the pure-white hero, the brand-graded hero, the three ratio frames (1:1 / 4:5 / 9:16), the vector logo, the font direction, and the Firefly review board link.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon flagship: 20 connector calls, 16 distinct Adobe tools, spanning 4 tool groups (asset/storage, photo-raster masking, tonal/color, vector + board). It exercises a realistic e-com product pipeline (geometry fix -> auto-tone -> subject mask -> invert -> solid white fill -> tight crop -> warm/HSL/contrast grade -> three honest ratio crops -> logo vectorize -> font + board) with a deep dependency chain where the final review board only exists because every upstream frame was produced. Difficulty comes from sequencing the mask/invert/fill correctly (white-bg the catalog way, not generative), keeping the grade on a fixed brand palette, and honestly cropping/reframing the graded hero to each target ratio (accepting edge/background loss) rather than inventing canvas. It deliberately stays inside Adobe headless capability: no text baked on (typeset handoff acknowledged + font_recommend given), no compositing, no gen-fill/gen-expand/object-removal, fill_area used only as a solid-color fill of a selection within the existing canvas (the white-bg step), and each ratio produced by a plain crop/resize of the existing frame.

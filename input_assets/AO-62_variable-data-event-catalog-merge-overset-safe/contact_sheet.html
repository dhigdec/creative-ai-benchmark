Party, Events & Promotion ready for agent brand-kit consistent

Run our variable-data merge into the auto-fit (shrink-to-fit) InDesign catalog template and the Illustrator price-tag template so long product names no longer overset, prepping the product hero art and licensed background first, and export print-ready PDFs.

💰 ~$55 est.Upwork15 tool calls14 distinct tools
Full client brief
We print a per-event promotional product catalog and matching shelf price tags, and our records come from a spreadsheet (one row per SKU: product name, blurb, price, event, SKU). Our recurring headache is that when a product name or blurb is long, the merged text overflows the frame and gets clipped on export — InDesign won't shrink it to fit on its own. We have already rebuilt our InDesign catalog template (catalog_template.indd) and our Illustrator price-tag template (pricetag_template.ai) with auto-size / "shrink text to fit frame" enabled on every variable-data frame and real merge fields (<<ProductName>>, <<Blurb>>, <<Price>>, <<SKU>>, <<EventName>>) wired up, so the layout itself is now overset-safe. We need you to: (1) stand up an asset workspace; (2) take in our authored templates, our catalog data (catalog_data.csv, ~24 SKUs), our product hero photo (hero_product.jpg) and our logo (logo_master.png); (3) prep the art that flows into the merge — isolate the product from its messy shot, clean the tone, build a clean bleed-safe background by cropping a licensed stock texture to the A4-plus-bleed frame, and vectorize the logo so it stays crisp at any print size; (4) run the data merge into BOTH the catalog .indd (one catalog card per row) and the price-tag .ai (Illustrator Variables, one tag per row); (5) export both as print-ready PDFs (catalog at A4 with bleed, tags at tag size); and (6) hand back the finished print-ready files. The whole point: long records must render legibly via the template's auto-fit, never clipped.

Why it's non-trivial

It exercises three independent prep branches (stock→reframe-crop→crop, cutout→tone→vibrance→crop, vectorize) that converge into a two-format variable-data merge, then export. Difficulty comes from (a) honoring the [T] reality — the merges only work because the client authored real .indd/.ai templates with bindable fields/variables AND the auto-fit/shrink-to-fit that solves the brief's overset complaint, so the agent must recognize it cannot 'fix overset' itself and must route field-matched CSV/art into the authored template; (b) exact field-name/column alignment between CSV, .indd fields and .ai variables; (c) the generative step being legitimately only image_crop_and_resize (reframe-crop), with no gen-fill/object-removal/compositing anywhere; (d) licensing stock BEFORE editing. The deliverable genuinely emerges from the chain rather than any single call.

**Template Description: `pricetag_template.ai`**

This is a client-supplied Adobe Illustrator (.ai) template designed for a 70x40mm shelf price tag.

**Structure & Layers:**
The template is a vector-based Illustrator document, implying distinct layers for background, text, and image elements. Its structure is optimized for dynamic content population via Illustrator Variables.

**Dimensions:**
70mm (width) x 40mm (height).

**Placeholders & Binding:**
The template features specific dynamic objects bound to Illustrator Variables, ensuring compatibility with data merge operations where variable names match CSV column headers:
*   `ProductName` (Text Placeholder): An area-type text object set with 'Auto Size' to automatically scale font size to fit longer product names within the designated area.
*   `Price` (Text Placeholder): A text object for displaying product pricing.
*   `SKU` (Text Placeholder): A text object for displaying the Stock Keeping Unit.
*   `Logo` (Image Placeholder): A linked-image variable placeholder for dynamically inserting a brand or product logo.

**Brand Styling:**
As an Illustrator document, the template inherently carries predefined brand styling including specific fonts, colors, and overall layout, designed to maintain consistent visual identity for the price tags.

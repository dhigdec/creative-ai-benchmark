Full client brief
Our small skincare-and-homeware shop is scaling onto Amazon, Etsy and our own Shopify store and we badly need visual credibility — right now our listings use phone snaps shot on a kitchen table and they look amateur, which is killing conversion. We can't afford a studio day, so we're sending you what we have: RAW product captures (a 24MP amber-glass serum bottle shot handheld under window light on a cluttered wooden table, slightly tilted) and a rough logo: our founder hand-inked our brand mark "AURELIA BOTANICALS" on paper and we photographed it with a phone — it's wonky, grey-ish, on off-white paper.

We need you to do the full product-photographer post-production pass and the logo cleanup:

PRODUCT IMAGE (do this as a repeatable recipe — we'll send 10-15 more bottles and jars that all must match):
- Level the horizon (the table edge is tilted).
- Cut the bottle off the messy table and put it on a PURE WHITE (#FFFFFF) seamless background, which is the hard requirement for Amazon's main-image policy.
- Deliver it as a square 2000×2000 px marketplace canvas with the product centered.
- Make it look professional: balanced exposure and color, true-to-life pop on the amber glass, and recover the blown-out highlights/hot spots from the window light so the glass reads cleanly.

LOGO:
- Take our phone photo of the inked sketch and turn it into a CLEAN, infinitely scalable vector (SVG) so it works on a 16px favicon and a 3-metre trade-show banner alike.
- Recolor the mark to our brand green (deep botanical green ~#1E5E3A).
- Recommend 2-3 fonts that pair with the hand-drawn mark for the "AURELIA BOTANICALS" wordmark so we can set the lockup ourselves.

DELIVERY: put the final catalog image, the transparent-background product cut-out, the vector logo and the recolored logo into a clean, clearly-named delivery folder we can pull straight into our listings and brand kit. Keep the white truly white — no grey cast — and keep the logo edges crisp at any size.

Workflow — 17 tool steps
  1. asset_initialize_file_upload — Begin upload of the client's raw 24MP serum-bottle capture into CC storage; returns presigned target + asset handle.
  2. asset_finalize_file_upload — Finalize the upload to get a stable CC asset URI for the raw bottle photo — the root input the whole product chain consumes.
  3. image_auto_straighten — Level the ~4-degree tilt (the diagonal table edge) so the bottle stands upright before masking.
  4. image_remove_background — Cut the bottle off the cluttered table → transparent PNG. This is deliverable AURELIA_serum_cutout_transparent.png and the source of the subject mask.
  5. image_invert_selection — Invert the subject mask so the (now empty) background region is selected, ready for a solid fill.
  6. image_fill_area — Solid-fill the inverted background selection with pure white #FFFFFF (solid-color fill only — the Amazon main-image white). No generative content.
  7. image_crop_and_resize — Crop/resize to a centered square 2000×2000 px marketplace canvas.
  8. image_apply_auto_tone — Balance global exposure and color cast from the bad window light.
  9. image_adjust_vibrance_and_saturation — Add true-to-life pop to the amber glass without oversaturating the white sweep.
  10. image_adjust_highlights — Recover the blown specular hot spots on the curved glass so it reads cleanly — final catalog hero deliverable and the repeatable recipe for the other 10-15 SKUs
  11. asset_initialize_file_upload — Begin upload of the founder's phone photo of the hand-inked logo sketch.
  12. asset_finalize_file_upload — Finalize to a stable CC asset URI for the raw logo sketch.
  13. image_vectorize — Trace the grey-ish raster ink sketch into a clean scalable SVG with crisp paths — favicon-to-banner safe. First logo deliverable.
  14. image_apply_color_overlay — Recolor the traced mark to brand botanical green ~#1E5E3A via solid color overlay — second logo deliverable.
  15. font_recommend — Recommend 2-3 wordmark fonts that pair with the recolored hand-drawn mark for the 'AURELIA BOTANICALS' lockup.
  16. asset_create_folders — Create the clearly-named client delivery folder (e.g. /AURELIA_Delivery/catalog + /brand) in CC storage.
  17. asset_copy_assets — Collate the catalog hero, the transparent cut-out, and both SVG logos into the delivery folder — ready to pull into listings and the brand kit.
Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 17 connector calls, 15 distinct Adobe tools (>> the ≥10 / ≥6 floors), spanning four capability groups — asset I/O (init/finalize/folders/copy), masking (remove_background → invert → solid fill), tonal/color (auto_tone, vibrance, highlights, color_overlay), geometry (auto_straighten, crop_and_resize), vector (vectorize) and type (font_recommend). It mirrors a real freelance product-photographer post-production + logo-cleanup job end to end. Every step is execution-mode [C] HEADLESS-CONFIRMED — no [W] Express widget, no [A] async video/audio, no [T] authored template, no [L] local step, and zero not-available capabilities: the white background is a solid #FFFFFF fill of an inverted mask (NOT generative fill or bg-replace-by-prompt), the logo is TRACED from a supplied raster sketch (NOT text-to-image or bespoke type drawing), the raster sketch is vectorized ONCE into a true SVG and the brand-green mark is a solid color overlay applied to that SVG (so the deliverable stays a genuine vector), and the wordmark is only a font RECOMMENDATION, not a composed lockup. Difficulty comes from correct ordering (straighten before mask; remove_background before invert; fill before crop; vectorize before recolor) and the mask hand-off at step 6, not from any disallowed magic.

Full client brief
Apexguard Auto Film, a fictional performance-automotive brand selling paint protection film (PPF) and window tint, is rebranding and needs its new identity turned into production-ready vector artwork and a print-ready vinyl decal sheet for its shop glass, product boxes, and installer-kit labels. The client has drafted the concepts with an AI image tool and only has flat raster PNGs: a master wordmark lockup for 'Apexguard' (a clean modern condensed sans in matte black with a single deep-blue accent chevron, premium/minimalist, no gradients), a sub-brand mark for 'Apexguard SHIELD' (the PPF line — the wordmark plus a simple hex-shield glyph), and a sub-brand mark for 'Apexguard TINT' (the window-tint line — the wordmark plus a tint-bar glyph rendered as flat bands). All three rasters carry the usual AI-export problems: soft anti-aliased edges, a faint off-white textured card behind the art, mild luminance noise, and each is tilted a couple of degrees off level. They cannot go to a vinyl cutter/printer as-is because they do not scale, have no clean cut path, and have no transparency. The client also supplies (a) an authored die-cut decal-sheet template (decal_sheet_template.ai) laid out on a single sheet with several registered sticker cells at different sizes — small installer-kit labels up to a large window/glass piece — each cell carrying a real placement/merge field plus a registered cut-line and bleed, and (b) a CSV of the approved sticker sizes and cell labels (cell_label, size_in, which_mark, mark) that matches the template's merge fields.

**Template Description:**

**File Name:** `decal_sheet_template.ai`

**Purpose:** Adobe Illustrator template for a multi-decal die-cut vinyl sheet layout, meticulously authored for automated vector data merge and precise cutting. This template ensures proper binding of vector merge data to cut paths, critical for print-shop production.

**Dimensions:**
*   **Artboard:** 24" x 18" (standard sheet size for vinyl printing and cutting).
*   **Decal Cells:**
    *   **Large (1x):** Approx. 10" x 8" (e.g., window/glass decal).
    *   **Medium (2x):** Approx. 4" x 4" each (e.g., box labels).
    *   **Small (6x):** Approx. 1.5" x 1.5" each (e.g., installer-kit labels).

**Structure & Layers:**
The document is precisely structured with dedicated layers for production efficiency:
*   **`Artwork` Layer:** Contains all live vector data-merge fields and visual elements.
*   **`CutContour` Layer (Spot Color):** Dedicated, non-printing spot color layer (e.g., "CutContour" or "Thru-Cut") holding all vector die-cut paths for each decal.
*   **`Bleed` Layer:** Guides and visual markers indicating the required bleed allowance around each decal cell.
*   **`TrimMarks` Layer:** Standard printer's trim and registration marks, positioned for accurate sheet alignment and cutting.
*   **`Guides` Layer (Locked):** Informational guides for layout, spacing, and safe zones.

**Placeholders & Styling:**
*   **Vector Data-Merge Fields:** Each decal cell contains a unique, live vector data-merge placeholder, such as `<<large_mark>>`, `<<medium_mark>>`, and `<<small_mark>>`. These fields are configured to dynamically import and place vector assets (e.g., logos, QR codes, part numbers) from a data source directly into the corresponding decal cell.
*   **Bleed Allowance:** Each decal cell includes a standard 0.125" bleed beyond the cut line, ensuring no unprinted edges after trimming.
*   **Cut-Line Registration:** The vector cut-paths on the `CutContour` layer are precisely registered to their respective artwork and merge fields, guaranteeing accurate die-cutting of the merged artwork.
*   **Brand Styling:** Follows standard print production best practices, including clear layer naming, spot color definition for cut paths, and precise vector construction, ensuring compatibility with automated cutting equipment.

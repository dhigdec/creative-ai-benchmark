Full client brief
Velvet Hour Sessions is a fictional intimate live-music night run by the Velvet Hour Room, a small arts-and-culture venue that films one acoustic set a month for its social channels (Instagram, Facebook, YouTube). Last Thursday we filmed the singer-songwriter 'Marlowe Fenn' doing a four-song stripped-back set on three cameras — a locked wide, a roaming handheld, and a close-up on the guitar/hands — and the card dump gave us a folder of a dozen-ish raw clips of very mixed quality. Before our editor builds the actual cut we need a fresh, disciplined pair of eyes to do the footage-review pass first: watch every raw clip end to end, decide which takes are genuinely usable and which are junk, and hand us back a selection log we can build the timeline from. Do NOT cut, grade, add music, or produce a finished video — this is a review-and-select job, exactly the first stage of the edit. You will receive: (1) velvet_hour_raw_clips (the folder of raw concert clips — some are clean keepers, several are unusable: one badly out of focus during the second song, one with a big camera bump / lens smack, one that's mostly dead air and tuning chatter before the song starts, one near-duplicate of a better wide take, and one where the handheld drifts off the performer for half the clip), (2) shoot_notes.csv (the runner's rough log: camera, rough song, and a one-line note per clip like 'wide, song 2, soft?' or 'handheld, song 4, drifts'), and (3) velvet_hour_brand_note.pdf (a short one-pager on what the venue's social edits are for and the 'clean, coherent, story-first, no gimmicks' house style so your keep/cut calls match how the footage will actually be used). Go through each clip against the notes and against what you actually see and hear, and return a shot-selection log that, for every clip, gives: the clip name, which camera/song it is, a KEEP / MAYBE / REJECT verdict, a plain-language reason (what's good or what's wrong — soft focus, camera bump, dead air, drift, redundant, clean), a usability rating (1-5), a note on the strongest usable stretch of the clip described in words (e.g. 'best from the first chorus onward, once focus settles'), and a coverage tag (wide / handheld / close-up) so we can see we have each angle for each song. Finish with a short summary: total clips, how many KEEP vs MAYBE vs REJECT, any song or angle where we're now short on usable coverage, and a one-line recommendation on whether we have enough to build the cut or need a re-shoot of anything. Deliver the log as a clean readable PDF plus the raw findings as a structured data file, and include a contact-sheet PDF showing one representative frame from each KEEP/MAYBE clip (with the clip name under each) so the director can eyeball the selects at a glance.

Full client brief
OVERDRIVE is a new scholarship-focused merchandise brand (education support via merch sales + scholarship-style campaigns); the look must read bold, modern, youthful, collegiate and merch-ready — premium streetwear, not charity/nonprofit — with the name shown as OVERDRIVE in all caps and a clean, scalable graduation-cap element (no government/university seal, lottery, or generic-education-clipart vibe). The client has approved a rendered raster logo lockup (primary horizontal version on a flat background). Your job, executed entirely with the Adobe connector, is to finish that concept into the production brand-kit deliverable set the client requested: (1) upload the supplied logo lockup into Adobe storage; (2) strip its background to a clean transparent primary logo PNG; (3) derive a tight-cropped icon/avatar from the cap mark and a square social-profile-picture crop sized for PFPs; (4) generate the merch-critical single-color variants — a full BLACK version and a full WHITE version (for dark/light garments, stickers, and watermarks) using subject selection + solid fills, plus one monochromatic brand-tint alt; (5) vectorize the transparent logo into a clean, scalable SVG source for hoodies/hats/large print; (6) produce a flat palette/swatch reference for the color-palette deliverable; and (7) recommend brand fonts that match the collegiate-streetwear tone. Hard exclusions per Adobe capability ground truth: no from-scratch logo generation, no AI object removal, no compositing, no upscaling — the supplied lockup is the source of truth and every deliverable is derived from it by chaining. The brief's requested AI/EPS source and multipage PDF, and the written originality/commercial-rights confirmation, are noted as human/format-export finishing items layered on top of the connector-produced SVG + PNG masters.

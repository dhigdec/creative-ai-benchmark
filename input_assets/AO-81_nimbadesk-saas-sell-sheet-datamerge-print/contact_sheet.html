Nimbadesk, a B2B SaaS company that sells a customer-support help-desk platform, needs its product sell sheets finished for print and for the sales team's leave-behind kit. Our production designer has already authored the layout: a one-page US-Letter (8.5" x 11" portrait) sell-sheet InDesign template (nimbadesk_sellsheet.indd) built on a clean modern grid, with a branded header, a "Why teams switch to Nimbadesk" company-advantage section, a core-features bullet block, a prominent "Book a demo" CTA, and real InDesign data-merge fields (<<tier_name>>, <<tagline>>, <<price_line>>, <<feature_1>>, <<feature_2>>, <<feature_3>>, <<cta_line>>) plus a hero image frame for the product screenshot and a logo frame. This is a production/finishing job, NOT a fresh branding exercise. We will hand off: the authored .indd template, a brand kit (hex palette #0E1F3D deep navy + #3B82F6 signal blue + #F5F7FA fog-grey, typography notes), three raw product-UI screenshots (the ticket inbox, the analytics dashboard, the automation builder) that were captured on-screen and look dull, slightly tilted and cool/washed-out, the Nimbadesk logo as a flat PNG, and a CSV holding one row per product tier (Starter, Growth, Enterprise) with the tier name, tagline, price line, three feature bullets and CTA line. The job: (1) make each product screenshot look crisp and on-brand — straighten it, auto-tone to fix the washed-out capture, warm/neutralize the cool cast, lift the shadows so dark-UI detail reads, nudge brightness/contrast, and lay a subtle signal-blue monochromatic tint so the three shots feel like one brand family; consistently crop all three to the same 16:10 ratio the hero frame expects; (2) vectorize the flat logo PNG into a clean SVG so the wordmark stays razor-sharp at print size and on the large header; (3) upload all the prepped assets, then run the client's authored .indd through InDesign data-merge against the tier CSV so each tier's copy binds into its own page of the template with the matching prepped screenshot in the hero frame and the vector logo in the header; (4) export the merged multi-page document to a print-ready PDF and a high-resolution JPEG deliverable. Keep everything on the supplied palette; the copy is exactly what the CSV carries — do not rewrite it. Final files must be print-ready (~300 DPI, US Letter portrait).

**Template File:** `nimbadesk_sellsheet.indd`

**Description:**
This is an Adobe InDesign template for a one-page B2B SaaS product sell sheet, formatted for US Letter (8.5" x 11") in portrait orientation. It features a clean, modern grid layout for a professional aesthetic.

**Structure & Layout:**
The template is organized into distinct sections:
1.  **Header:** Branded section containing the company logo frame and key product identifiers.
2.  **Hero Image:** A prominent image frame (16:10 ratio) intended for a product screenshot.
3.  **Company Advantage:** A dedicated block titled 'Why teams switch to Nimbadesk', detailing company benefits.
4.  **Core Features:** A three-bullet list highlighting primary product features.
5.  **Call to Action (CTA) Band:** A prominent footer band for the main call to action.

**Layers:**
The document is typically structured with a 'Background/Graphics' layer for fixed brand elements and a 'Content' layer for editable text and image frames.

**Placeholders:**
The template contains genuine InDesign data-merge placeholder fields for dynamic content population:
*   `<<tier_name>>` (in header)
*   `<<tagline>>` (in header)
*   `<<price_line>>` (within product details)
*   `<<feature_1>>`, `<<feature_2>>`, `<<feature_3>>` (for the core features list)
*   `<<cta_line>>` (in the CTA band)
Additionally, dedicated frames are present for the company logo (header) and the hero product screenshot.

**Brand Styling:**
The design adheres to the Nimbadesk brand guidelines, utilizing a palette of #0E1F3D (navy), #3B82F6 (signal blue), and #F5F7FA (fog-grey) for a consistent and modern appearance. Typography is clean and legible, complementing the grid-based layout.

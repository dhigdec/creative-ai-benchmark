Full client brief
Summit & Sable is a direct-to-consumer overland and off-road recovery gear brand refreshing its online catalog for the new season. We are NOT reshooting — we need our existing product photos cleaned up and made consistent, plus a couple of color variants generated from shots we already have, exactly the way our old Photoshop editor used to do it. We're handing off six existing product photos (JPEG, roughly 2400x3000, shot in mixed lighting across two sessions so they don't match): a red recovery strap coiled on white sweep, the same strap's anodized aluminum snatch-hook shackle in close-up, a soft shackle, a folding shovel, a recovery tracks pair, and a branded ratchet strap with our printed label visible. Two jobs. First, batch grade: run all six through one consistent HOUSE LOOK so the catalog reads as a matched set — auto-tone the flat ones, correct the cool/warm white-balance mismatch between the two sessions to a neutral daylight, even out exposure, add a touch of vibrance so the products pop on a white PDP background, and lock a single Lightroom-style preset across the batch so contrast and tone are identical. Do not blow out the whites or crush the blacks; catalog images must stay true-to-product. Second, color variants WITHOUT a reshoot: on the recovery-strap hero, swap the strap-hardware accent from our current safety-orange anodizing to a gunmetal/graphite finish so we can list a second colorway; and on the ratchet-strap shot, shift the printed label's red brand block to our new forest-green house color. THE CATCH (this is what our last editor got wrong): change the color without ruining the shot — the lighting, shadows, metal reflections and woven-webbing texture must remain completely natural. No sloppy masking, no flat dead-fill color, no unrealistic edits; the recolor has to sit under the existing highlights and shadows. Deliverables: the six graded catalog JPEGs at web resolution (long edge 2000px, sRGB), the two recolored variant images, and a single review board deep-link so our merchandiser can approve the house look and both colorways in one place before they go on the site.

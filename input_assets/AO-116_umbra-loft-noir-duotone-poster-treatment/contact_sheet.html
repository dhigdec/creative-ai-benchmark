Full client brief
Umbra Loft, a fictional basement live-jazz and spoken-word venue, is announcing its autumn "Midnight Sessions" concert series and wants a cohesive poster look built entirely from its own archive. They are handing off three raw archival performance photos pulled from past nights: a stand-up double-bass player under a single stage spot, a vocalist leaning into a vintage ribbon mic, and a wide shot of the trio mid-set on the low stage. As-shot the photos are unusable for a series: each is flat and dull, shot on a phone in near-dark with mixed warm house light and a cool spill from an EXIT sign, slightly tilted, with a cluttered background of cabling, monitors and audience heads. The venue's whole identity is dark, moody and cinematic, and they want the three photos driven to one signature NOIR TWO-TONE (duotone) treatment so they read instantly as a matched set at poster size on the venue wall and at Instagram-thumbnail size in the feed: deep ink-blue shadows lifting to a warm amber highlight, a subtle print-screen halftone texture so it feels like a screened gig poster, fine film grain so the flat inks don't band on large print, and darkened edges so the eye is pulled to the performer. The look must be applied IDENTICALLY to all three so the set is consistent. The job, per photo: (1) straighten the tilted frame; (2) auto-tone the flat near-dark exposure and push contrast hard so the two-tone mapping has clean separation between the lit performer and the dark room; (3) desaturate to a neutral grayscale base to kill the mixed warm/cool house-light cast; (4) flatten the grayscale to a single-ink monochromatic base tint in the deep ink-blue shadow ink across the whole frame; (5) lay the warm amber highlight ink over the whole ink-blue base as a whole-image color overlay in a lighten/screen blend so only the brighter (lit-performer) pixels shift toward amber, producing the two-ink noir duotone; (6) apply a subtle whole-image halftone dot texture for the screened-poster feel; (7) add fine film grain; (8) darken the frame edges toward the ink shadow with a whole-image color overlay in a multiply/darken blend so the composition vignettes down and the performer reads as hero; (9) crop to the venue's A2 poster ratio; and separately (10) crop the same treated frame to a 1:1 Instagram tile. Deliver, for each of the three photos, a print-ready high-resolution poster-ratio image (~300 DPI equivalent) and a 1:1 social crop; then package the full treated set into a single zip and assemble the treated posters onto a Firefly board so the venue can sign off on the whole series look in one deep-link. No text, logo, or layout is required in these deliverables — this is the photographic treatment only; the venue will drop the finished duotone images into their own poster te

AO-12 — Bright-and-Airy Lightroom Grade for a 38-Shot Food Photography Set (Flat-Lay, 45°, Side)

Food, Restaurant & Beverage ready for agent brand-kit consistent

Take a client's 38-frame food shoot (flat-lay / 45° / side of one dish) that is close to the mark and grade it to a cohesive bright-and-airy look — white balance, exposure, highlight/shadow, gentle color — then deliver straightened, correctly-sized final files.

Full client brief
A food-photography client (single hero dish, shot three ways: an overhead flat-lay, a 45-degree three-quarter, and a side/profile shot, 38 frames total) hands you a set that is "close to the goal but needs tweaking." The goal is a consistent BRIGHT AND AIRY look across the whole set: clean neutral-to-slightly-warm white balance, lifted exposure, bright but not blown highlights on the plate and window light, open (not muddy) shadows, and gentle, appetising color — applied cohesively so the 38 frames read as one shoot. Quick turnaround / ASAP.\n\nConcretely, working in the Adobe connector entirely on the client's own pixels (no compositing, no generative fill, no background swap):\n1. Ingest the three representative hero frames the recipe is tuned on.\n2. On the flat-lay, square it up (auto-straighten the overhead so the plate edges are true) and crop to a clean composition.\n3. Build the bright-and-airy base grade on that straightened flat-lay: warm the white balance toward a clean daylight, lift exposure, pull the brightest highlights back so the window light and the plate rim are bright-not-blown, open the dark portions so the shadows are airy, then a restrained vibrance/saturation bump and a subtle color-temperature confirmation.\n4. Selectively brighten the dish itself: prompt-select the food, invert to protect the background, and give the food a small targeted highlight/exposure lift so the hero pops without crushing the airy backdrop.\n5. Carry the identical look onto the 45-degree frame and the side frame (auto-tone to seat them, then the same warm-WB + highlight-recovery + shadow-lift recipe) so all three — and by extension the remaining 35 — match.\n6. Produce delivery-ready files: crop/resize the graded frames to the client's delivery dimensions, and size one of them for an Instagram social teaser.\n\nDeliverables: the three fully-graded hero frames at delivery resolution, the selective-food-pop master, an Instagram-sized teaser, and a short edit recipe note describing the cohesive look so the client can see exactly how the set was unified.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon flagship: 21 steps, 16 distinct Adobe tools, spanning three connector groups (asset/stock, the raster photo family, and Firefly boards) plus one allowed local packaging step. It is genuinely hard because the agent must (a) sequence a real Lightroom bright-and-airy recipe in the correct tonal order (straighten/crop before grading; WB→exposure→highlight recovery→shadow lift→vibrance, not arbitrary), (b) build and correctly invert a prompt-mask to pop the dish while protecting the airy background, and (c) MATCH that look across two more angles via auto-tone-seated sub-chains so the set reads cohesive — the core ask of the brief.

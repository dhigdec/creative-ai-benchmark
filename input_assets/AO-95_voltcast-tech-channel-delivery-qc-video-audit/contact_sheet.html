Full client brief
Voltcast is a fictional independent consumer-tech review channel run by Voltcast Media that publishes several times a week -- long-form reviews plus short vertical cuts for Shorts / Reels / TikTok. We outsource the actual editing to a rotating pool of junior editors, and before an episode goes live we run a delivery QC pass so nothing off-spec reaches the feed (the last episode published with the sponsor read buried under the music and the price-callout overlay clipped off the bottom on mobile, and we only caught it after it went out).

For this episode ('Voltcast E42 -- Budget Phone Shootout') you will receive four things: (1) voltcast_e42_master_16x9.mp4, the finished 4K 16:9 YouTube master the editor delivered; (2) voltcast_e42_vertical_9x16.mp4, the reformatted vertical Shorts/Reels cut derived from the same edit; (3) voltcast_delivery_spec.pdf, our written channel delivery-spec sheet; and (4) voltcast_brand_frames.png, a reference sheet of our correct intro end-card, lower-third, and price-callout overlay so you can compare on-screen text against the approved look. The delivery-spec sheet states the exact rules: long-form runtime must land in the 8:00-12:00 window; master must be 3840x2160 at 23.976 or 24 fps, H.264, with integrated loudness at -14 LUFS (+/-1) and true-peak at or below -1 dBTP; background music must sit at least 12 LU below narration during any spoken segment (no clashing under the sponsor read or verdict); the branded intro end-card must appear in the first 8 seconds and the subscribe/outro card in the final 15 seconds; every text overlay (lower-thirds, spec/price callouts) must sit inside the title-safe margin (5% inset) so it is not clipped, and the vertical 9:16 cut must keep those callouts inside the tighter mobile safe area; on-brand text must use the channel's approved lower-third style, not a default editor font.

The job is an audit, not a re-edit -- do NOT re-cut, re-grade, re-mix, or re-export the episode. Watch/inspect both deliverables, compare what is actually there against the spec sheet, and hand back a delivery-QC report that, for each issue found, gives the affected file, the timecode (or time range), a plain-language description of the drift, the exact spec clause it violates, the observed-vs-required value, and a severity (blocker / warning / nit). We expect you to catch things like: a runtime that overshoots the 12:00 ceiling, music that does NOT drop under the sponsor read, a price-callout overlay clipped outside the mobile safe area on the vertical cut, an outro card that is missing or too short, and a lower-third set in the wrong font.

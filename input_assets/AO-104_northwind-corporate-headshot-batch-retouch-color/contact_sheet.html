Full client brief
Hi — this is Priya Venkataraman, office manager at Northwind Advisory Partners (a boutique wealth-advisory firm). We had our new partner, Daniel Okoro, sit for a classic corporate head-shot last week and the photographer gave us the un-retouched exports, but we need someone to actually finish them. Please work only from the files I'm attaching: three high-res studio frames of Daniel against our neutral light-grey backdrop (slightly different poses/expressions), plus a one-page retouch brief with our house rules.

We want a traditional, formal look — nothing trendy. Specifically:
1. Clean up and level each of the three frames (they're a hair tilted), then apply a consistent professional studio tone so exposure and contrast look intentional across all three, not flat.
2. The studio strobes left everything slightly cool — warm the skin tone a touch and give the set a cohesive graded look so all three read as one session. Consistency across the three is the whole point; they'll sit together on the "Our Team" page.
3. Tasteful, believable retouching only: gently brighten his eyes and teeth using selective adjustments, and even out the light on his face. No plastic skin, no reshaping, no changing the backdrop — the light-grey seamless stays exactly as shot.
4. Deliver each finished colour portrait in the crops we'll actually use: a 1:1 LinkedIn/avatar crop (1200x1200), a 4:5 website "Our Team" portrait crop (1600x2000), and a 5:7 print crop (1500x2100) suitable for framing at reception.
5. For every finished colour portrait we also need a clean black-and-white version — a true, neutral monochrome conversion, not a heavy filter — in the same three crops.
6. Put everything into one delivery folder, organised so we can review the colour set and the B&W set side by side, and send me a preview link so Daniel and the partners can sign off.

Turnaround within the week, please. Deliverable: a high-resolution, print/web-ready corporate headshot package (colour + clean B&W, LinkedIn + website + print crops) for Daniel, consistent across all three source frames.

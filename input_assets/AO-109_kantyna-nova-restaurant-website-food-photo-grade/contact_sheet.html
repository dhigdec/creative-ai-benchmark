Full client brief
Kantyna Nova, a modern Polish comfort-food restaurant and catering brand, is launching a new website and needs its food photography brought up to a premium, cohesive standard. The owner, Marta Zawadzka, has handed off eight raw phone-and-DSLR shots straight from a shoot day: two hero plates (a bowl of pierogi with browned onions, a golden schnitzel platter), a bright beet-and-goat-cheese salad, a hearty bowl of zurek soup, a slice of layered sernik cheesecake, a flat-lay of a catering grazing spread, a bar shot of a craft kompot drink, and a wide interior shot of the dining room. As-shot they are inconsistent and unappetizing: several are slightly tilted, most are flat and underexposed with a cool/greenish canteen-lighting cast, backgrounds are busy, and the colours drift from photo to photo so they would never read as one brand. The job is a pure retouch/grade edit (no layout, no new imagery): (1) straighten anything tilted and auto-tone every shot to a clean baseline; (2) establish ONE cohesive premium look on the lead hero — warm the white balance off the canteen green, lift the shadows, push vibrance so food reads rich and appetizing, boost contrast, and settle on a reusable food-grade preset so the whole set can be aligned to one named look; (3) apply that same preset across all remaining shots so the full set looks graded together, with per-shot exposure nudges where a frame is darker; (4) throw the busiest backgrounds gently out of focus with a subject-aware background depth-of-field lens blur (keeps the plate/glass sharp, softens the clutter behind it) so the food/subject reads as hero while keeping a natural look; (5) crop-and-resize each graded image into TWO matched deliverables — a desktop hero crop at 16:9 (1920x1080) and a mobile crop at 4:5 (1080x1350) — so the site has correctly-composed assets for both breakpoints without stretching. Keep everything natural — no plastic over-processing. Deliverables: sixteen graded, correctly-cropped JPEGs (8 photos x desktop + mobile), organised in a clean folder structure (desktop/ and mobile/ subfolders) for hand-off. Brand direction: warm, rich, premium, appetizing; consistent grade across every image; natural, not garish.

KANTYNA NOVA: BRAND PHOTO DIRECTION

**Client:** Marta Zawadzka
**Brand:** Kantyna Nova (Modern Polish Comfort Food & Catering)

**OVERALL AESTHETIC GOAL:**
To visually represent Kantyna Nova as a warm, inviting, and contemporary Polish comfort food experience. Images should evoke a sense of deliciousness, authenticity, and approachable quality, moving away from dated or 'canteen-like' perceptions. We aim for an elevated yet natural feel.

**PHOTOGRAPHIC DIRECTIVES:**

*   **White Balance & Color:** Crucially, correct any cool, drab 'canteen-green' cast often associated with institutional kitchens. Aim for a distinctly warm and rich white balance. Colors should feel inviting and true to life, enhancing the natural appeal of the food and environment.
*   **Shadows & Light:** Shadows should be gently lifted, ensuring detail and warmth are retained without flattening the image. Avoid harsh, deep shadows. Light should feel natural and soft, suggestive of a welcoming space rather than a studio setup.
*   **Vibrance & Saturation:** Elevate vibrance to make food look exceptionally appetizing and fresh. Colors should pop just enough to be enticing, but without appearing oversaturated or artificial.
*   **Contrast:** Apply gentle contrast. We want depth and visual interest, but avoid overly dramatic or harsh contrast that can make food look uninviting or processed.
*   **Processing & Grade:** The overall look must be natural and authentic, not over-processed or overly stylized. A consistent grade across all images is paramount to ensure brand cohesion. Each image should feel like part of a unified visual story for Kantyna Nova.

**TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS:**

*   **Desktop Hero Crops:** All primary hero images for desktop use should be delivered at a 16:9 aspect ratio, specifically 1920x1080 pixels.
*   **Mobile Crops:** All primary images for mobile use should be delivered at a 4:5 aspect ratio, specifically 1080x1350 pixels.

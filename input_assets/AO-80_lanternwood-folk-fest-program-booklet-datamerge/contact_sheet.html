Full client brief
Lanternwood Folk Fest, a fictional two-day roots-and-Americana music festival, needs its official program booklet finished for print and for the event app. The organizer has handed off an authored multi-page InDesign booklet template (folkfest_program_template.indd) that already carries the festival's established warm-cream / lantern-amber colour scheme and type hierarchy, with real InDesign data-merge fields laid out across the interior spreads (<<day>>, <<stage>>, <<set_time>>, <<artist>>, <<bio>>, <<artist_photo>> for the schedule pages, and <<sponsor_name>>, <<sponsor_tier>>, <<sponsor_logo>> for the sponsor grid on the back-cover spread), plus front/back covers with a full-bleed frame for a kraft-paper texture and a placeholder for the festival wordmark. Along with the template the organizer supplies: a schedule CSV (one row per performance set: day, stage, set time, artist, one-line bio, and the filename of that artist's press photo), a sponsor CSV (sponsor name, tier — Presenting / Stage / Community — and the filename of that sponsor's logo), a folder of raw sponsor logo files (some are clean flat rasters, some are photos of the logo on a busy background, some are already reasonable PNGs), and a folder of raw performer press photos that are unappetizing as-shot: slightly tilted, dull and flat, mixed white-balance because they came from different photographers. The job: (1) make every sponsor logo crisp and print-safe per the brief — vectorize the flat raster logos into clean SVGs so they stay sharp at any size, and lift the busy-background logos onto clean transparent PNGs so they sit consistently on the cream page; (2) grade the performer press photos so the artists look their best — straighten the tilted ones, auto-tone the flat exposure, normalize the mixed white balance toward the festival's warm look, push vibrance, and crop each to the uniform portrait ratio the schedule frames expect; (3) license a subtle kraft/recycled-paper texture from Adobe Stock to back the front and back covers; (4) upload all the prepped assets, then run the client's authored .indd through the InDesign data-merge against the schedule CSV (interior schedule/bio pages) and then against the sponsor CSV (back-cover sponsor grid) so every set, bio, artist photo, sponsor name, tier and logo binds into the correct frame; (5) export the merged booklet to a press-ready CMYK PDF with bleed/crop marks for the printer and a lighter RGB screen-quality PDF for the event app. All copy stays exactly as supplied in the CSVs — the design work is the layout binding, image optimization and logo prep the brief asks for, nothing rewritten.

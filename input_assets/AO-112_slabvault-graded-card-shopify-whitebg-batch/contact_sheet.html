Full client brief
SlabVault Cards, an online collectibles store selling graded slabs and sealed hobby product, needs its new-inventory photos turned into clean, upload-ready Shopify images. The owner shot everything at the kitchen table on an iPhone, so the raw files are inconsistent: slightly tilted, dull and cool-cast under kitchen lighting, and sitting on a busy wood-grain table with a phone charger, a coffee ring, and other clutter in frame. There are six SKUs in this batch — a PSA 10 graded rookie slab, a PSA 9 vintage slab, a BGS 9.5 chrome refractor slab, a sealed hobby box, a sealed blaster box, and a three-card graded lot on a stand — each supplied as one hero photo, plus a CSV listing every SKU's Shopify product handle for file naming. The job, applied identically to all six so the storefront looks cohesive: (1) lift the product cleanly off the cluttered table so no wood grain, charger, or coffee ring survives — deliver a transparent PNG cutout of each item, and also place each cutout onto a clean, pure-white (#FFFFFF) background with no drop shadow and no grey gradient, just flat white as Shopify prefers; (2) straighten the tilt so every slab reads dead-level, and auto-tone plus gently warm the cool kitchen cast so the card faces and slab labels look true and consistent across the set; (3) center the product in frame with even margin and crop/resize every white-background image to Shopify's recommended 2048 x 2048 px square at consistent scale so the products don't jump size-to-size in the storefront grid; (4) name each exported file to match its Shopify product handle from the CSV so the owner can bulk-associate them on upload. Deliver the six finished 2048x2048 white-background JPEGs, keep the transparent PNG cutouts too (useful for marketplace cross-posting), stage everything into a dated Shopify-upload folder in Creative Cloud, and drop a short recipe note documenting the exact edit steps so the same look can be reproduced on the next inventory drop. These are collectibles worth real money, so slabs must be perfectly straight, centered, and shadow-free.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon batch: 37 connector calls, 10 distinct Adobe tools across three groups (image_* raster editing, asset_* storage/naming, local recipe note) — well above the >=10 calls / >=6 distinct-tool floor. Difficulty comes from running six separate SKUs (flat slabs, a reflective refractor, tall 3D boxes, a multi-object lot) through a consistent straighten -> auto-tone -> warm -> background-removal -> center-crop pipeline, holding scale and white-balance consistency across the whole set, branching each item into BOTH a transparent PNG cutout and a clean-white composite, then naming every export to its Shopify handle from a supplied CSV. It respects every hard limit: 'product on clean white' is produced the tool-sanctioned way via image_remove_background with options.backgroundColor='#FFFFFF' (a single call), NOT by feeding a transparent PNG into image_fill_area (which the tool docs explicitly warn against — wrong mask polarity — and which is the reason image_fill_area is deliberately unused here); image_crop_and_resize sets onSubjectClipping to 'pad' so it never emits a requires_expand_chain / image_crop_and_resize directive; no generative fill / generative-expand / reframe-crop, no text-to-image / from-scratch art, no upscaling, no object-removal-with-reconstruction; image_select_subject and image_select_by_prompt only INSPECT/preview the cutout region, they do not synthesize.

Full client brief
You are the production designer prepping the photo and brand assets that will be dropped into 12 static Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad mockups for a family-owned, authentic Mexican restaurant in West Palm Beach, FL. The brand is warm, bold, family-oriented and community-focused — a local neighborhood favorite, NOT a corporate chain. The campaign covers two offers: Taco Tuesday (scroll-stop, urgent, recurring-promo feel) and Lunch Specials. The client has supplied three raw phone/DSLR food photos (a hero al-pastor taco platter, a carne-asada lunch plate, a margarita-and-chips table scene), plus their logo as a flat PNG.

Your job, entirely within the Adobe connector:
1) Load the three supplied food photos and the logo PNG into the project.
2) Straighten and tonally fix the hero taco shot (auto-straighten the slightly tilted handheld frame, then auto-tone), so it reads clean and appetizing.
3) Push a warm, bold restaurant grade on the corrected hero: lift color temperature toward warm, boost vibrance/saturation so the salsa reds and lime greens pop, and recover highlight detail on the glossy meat — the "appetizing and bold" look the brand wants.
4) Build a scroll-stop "color-splash" variant from the graded hero: select the taco/food by prompt, invert the selection so everything BUT the food is selected, and desaturate that background so the food is the only color on screen (eye-catching scroll-stop power).
5) Apply an Adaptive "Subject Pop" preset to the graded hero to create a second high-contrast scroll-stop variant with the food made to stand forward.
6) Create a clean cut-out of the lunch plate: remove the background to a transparent PNG so the dish can float on any colored ad background the layout team chooses.
7) Reframe the graded hero into the three Meta ad canvases the campaign needs — square 1:1 feed, 4:5 portrait feed, and 9:16 story — by cropping and resizing the existing shot to each ratio/size (keep the food in frame, use the surrounding table/scene as the reframe margin; no scene is invented).
8) License one on-brand backdrop texture (warm woven Mexican textile / rustic wood) from Adobe Stock at full res for the layout team to use behind cut-outs.
9) Vectorize the supplied logo PNG into a clean SVG so it scales crisply across every ad size.
10) Assemble all produced assets into a Firefly board as a labeled review deck for the client, and copy the final delivery set into a clearly named project folder.

Deliverables: the corrected + warm-graded hero JPEG; the color-splash hero; the Subject-Pop hero; the transparent-PNG lunch-plate cut-out; three ratio-correct hero canvases (1:1, 4:5, 9:16); one licensed full-res backdrop texture; the logo SVG; a Firefly review board link; and a named delivery folder containing the set.

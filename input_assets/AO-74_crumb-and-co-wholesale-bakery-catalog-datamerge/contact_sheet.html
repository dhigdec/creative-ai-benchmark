Full client brief
Crumb & Co., a small-batch wholesale bakery, needs its B2B distributor catalog finished for print and PDF hand-out. The owner has already authored an InDesign catalog template (crumbco_catalog_template.indd) — an 8.5x11 portrait, multi-page grid layout with repeating product cells, each cell holding an image frame plus real InDesign data-merge fields (<<product_name>>, <<sku>>, <<description>>, <<case_pack>>, <<wholesale_price>>) and section headers for Breads, Pastries, Cakes & Slices, and Cookies & Bars, with a masthead frame for the wordmark and a full-bleed cover panel. The owner has also handed off the wholesale product roster as a CSV (12 SKUs across the four sections, each with a keyword-rich product description, case-pack quantity, and B2B wholesale price) and twelve raw product photos shot quickly on the bakery counter — sourdough boules, seeded rye, croissants, cinnamon rolls, a layer cake, brownies, chocolate-chip cookies, and more. The photos are unappetizing as-shot: slightly tilted, flat and dull, cool fluorescent white balance, and cluttered counter backgrounds. The job: (1) make every product photo look like a "high-quality image" — straighten the tilted frames, auto-tone to fix the flat exposure, warm the cool white balance toward a golden bakery look, push vibrance so crust and glaze read appetizing, apply a consistent bakery-warm preset across all twelve so they match, and crop every photo to the identical square (1:1) ratio the template's cells expect; (2) vectorize the flat Crumb & Co. wordmark PNG into a clean SVG so the masthead stays razor-sharp at print size; (3) upload all the prepped assets (twelve graded 1:1 photos, the vector wordmark) to Creative Cloud storage; (4) run the client's authored .indd through an InDesign data-merge against the roster CSV so every product cell binds its name, SKU, description, case pack, and wholesale price, with each graded photo dropping into its matching cell; (5) export the merged catalog to a print-ready ~300 DPI PDF for the print run and a high-resolution JPEG of the cover/spread for emailing to distributors. Deliverable dimensions: US Letter 8.5x11 portrait, print-ready CMYK-quality PDF plus a max-quality JPEG. Brand constraints: keep the warm/golden bakery palette consistent across every photo, the wordmark must stay crisp at masthead scale, and all descriptions/prices bind verbatim from the supplied roster CSV — do not rewrite the copy.

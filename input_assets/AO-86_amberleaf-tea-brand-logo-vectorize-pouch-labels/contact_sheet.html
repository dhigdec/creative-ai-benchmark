Full client brief
Amberleaf Tea Studio, a small independent tea-branding consultancy, is prepping two of its house tea brands for a production run and needs the artwork made print-ready. The studio has already generated the concept artwork with an AI image tool but only has flat raster PNGs: a wordmark lockup for 'Golden Chai Co.' (warm amber-and-cream with a subtle gold gradient), a wordmark lockup for 'Silverleaf Green' (cool sage-green with a fine leaf glyph), and one ornamental 'crest' mark (a symmetrical scrollwork badge with a small tea-leaf motif meant to sit on both brands' packaging). All three rasters have the usual AI-export problems: soft anti-aliased edges, a faint off-white textured card behind the art, mild luminance noise, and each is tilted a couple of degrees off level. None of these can go to a printer as-is because they do not scale and have no transparency. The studio also supplies a flat mockup image of a blank stand-up coffee/tea pouch it wants to use as the packaging shape. The job: (1) ingest all three raster logos and get clean working copies; auto-straighten each tilted mark and tonally clean it so edges are true and the near-white card is even; (2) crop each mark tight to its true artwork bounds and knock the background to transparent so the trace has no halo; (3) VECTORIZE each cleaned mark into a crisp, fully-scalable SVG master while preserving every colour and the gold gradient exactly as in the concept — these three SVGs are the primary editable/print-ready vector deliverables (AI/EPS/PDF are the same master exported to those formats); (4) from the ornamental crest, derive a single-color / knockout vector (crest silhouette solid-filled) for one-colour foil, embossing and small favicon use, and a halftone 'print-screen' treatment for merch and kraft-paper packaging; (5) produce the 'matching front-facing product label' the brief asks for: crop the supplied stand-up-pouch mockup to just its flat front panel and lay a clean solid kraft-tone base band on that panel, delivering the print-ready front-label base panel that fits the new vector style — the cleaned Golden Chai wordmark + crest are dropped onto it by the studio in the layered vector files; (6) recommend a brand-safe font pairing that matches the wordmark style so the studio can extend the identity across both brands; (7) upload every produced deliverable into a tidy Creative Cloud hand-off folder and assemble them onto a single Firefly board deep-link so the studio can approve the vectors and the pouch label in one place. Preserve every colour, gradient and detail exactly as in the original concept while improving line sharpness and overall balance; keep the two brands visually distinct.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon and wide-mix: 26 connector calls, 15 distinct Adobe tools across three groups (asset ingest/storage, the image_* raster/vector family, and font/board). Ordering matters — each mark must be straightened, tonally cleaned, cropped and background-knocked BEFORE vectorizing or the trace inherits the AI noise/tilt/halo and fails the 'clean, fully-editable, scale without loss' bar; a naive agent that vectorizes the raw PNGs fails. It runs three separate marks through their own consistent clean->trace pipelines (two wordmarks with opposite tilts + one filigree crest), then reuses the cleaned crest as a hub to spawn the one-colour knockout (color_overlay -> re-vectorize) and the halftone variant, and produces the front-facing label base from a crop + solid-fill branch. It respects every hard limit: no generative fill / object-removal / text-to-image / reframe-crop / upscaling / from-scratch layout / image-onto-image compositing; image_fill_area is used solid-colour ONLY (kraft base band, no generated content, no placement of other marks); the vector masters come solely from image_vectorize (raster->SVG), and AI/EPS/PDF are honestly described as export formats of that same master rather than authored by a from-scratch vector tool.

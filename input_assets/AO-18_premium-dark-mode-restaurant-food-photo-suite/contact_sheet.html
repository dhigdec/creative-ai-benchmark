AO-18 — Premium Dark-Mode Restaurant Food-Photography Asset Suite (Hero, Menu Tiles, Logo Vector)

Food, Restaurant & Beverage ready for agent brand-kit consistent

From the client's raw food photos and logo, produce a dark-mode-ready food-photography asset suite — a color-graded wide hero banner, clean-backdrop menu tiles, a mobile crop, and a vector logo — for a premium restaurant website.

💰 ~$138 est.Upwork26 tool calls14 distinct toolslong-horizon
Full client brief
We are a boutique digital agency building a premium, dark-mode restaurant website (Home, Interactive Food Menu, Contact/Booking) that focuses heavily on high-quality food photography and must be perfectly optimized for mobile. We are NOT asking you to build the site here — we have a Framer developer for that. What we need from YOU is the polished food-photography asset suite the design hangs on, all prepared from our raw shoot so it drops straight into the dark aesthetic.

Deliverables, all derived from the assets we supply:
1) HERO BANNER — take our raw hero shot (a signature octopus dish, slightly crooked off the handheld shoot) and turn it into a wide, cinematic, dark-mode hero: straighten it, apply a cohesive warm-but-moody grade so it reads premium on a near-black background, then crop it to a full ultra-wide 21:9 banner ratio (a cinematic letterbox reframe of the existing wide shot) so our developer has clean headline space. Mobile-optimized vertical crop of the same hero, too.
2) MENU TILES — for two menu items (the octopus and a steak plate), isolate the plated dish onto a clean, uniform near-black backdrop (so all menu tiles match), color-match them to the hero grade, and deliver square tiles.
3) LOGO MARK — our logo is supplied as a flat raster; we need a crisp vector version (SVG) for retina/responsive use across the dark site.

House look: warm highlights, deep controlled shadows, rich but not cartoonish saturation, consistent across every tile so the menu grid feels like one shoot. Keep everything cohesive — the whole point of the dark-mode design is that the photography carries it.
Deliverables (6)
  • hero_banner_ultrawide.png — 21:9 ultra-wide dark-mode hero banner, straightened + warm-moody graded, then cinematic letterbox reframe cropped from the existing wide 3:2 source (no invented/extended pixels), ~3440x1440 PNG, ready
  • hero_mobile_vertical.jpg — Mobile hero crop of the same graded hero, 4:5 portrait ~1080x1350 JPEG, subject-safe framing
  • menu_tile_octopus.jpg — Square menu tile, plated octopus isolated on uniform near-black (#0B0B0D) backdrop, grade-matched, ~1200x1200 JPEG
  • menu_tile_steak.jpg — Square menu tile, plated steak isolated on the SAME near-black (#0B0B0D) backdrop, color-matched to hero grade, ~1200x1200 JPEG
  • logo_mark.svg — Clean 2-color vector logo mark (monoline octopus + wordmark), scalable SVG for retina/responsive use
  • restaurant_photo_suite.zip — Single zip bundling all five deliverables in a /hero, /menu, /logo folder structure for handoff

Why it's non-trivial

Genuine output->input chaining with three real reuse points of the locked hero grade (banner, mobile, octopus tile), which is what makes it 'wide-mix' rather than a linear pipeline. Strictly inside the capability sheet: ZERO generative steps — the 21:9 hero reframe is a true image_crop_and_resize letterbox crop of the existing wide 3:2 pixels (downscaled to 3440x1440, never larger than source, no invented content); image_fill_area is used for solid near-black backdrop only (no gen-fill, no object removal, no compositing); selective warmth via adjust tools; image_vectorize is a legitimate raster->SVG trace; no layout COMPOSITION is claimed (document_render_* deliberately not used since there is no authored .indd/.ai).

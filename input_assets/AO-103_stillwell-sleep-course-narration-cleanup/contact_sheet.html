Full client brief
Stillwell is a small sleep-and-mindfulness education platform. We are building a self-paced course module titled 'Easing Insomnia: A Six-Lesson Wind-Down', and we recorded the narration ourselves at home. The problem is the audio: our host recorded the six lessons on a USB mic in a home office, so every take has steady room hiss, a faint HVAC hum, a bit of laptop-fan whine, and uneven levels where the voice drifts loud and soft. The script is finished and the delivery is warm and calm, which we love — we just need the VOICE cleaned up so it is clear, consistent, and soothing enough for people to fall asleep to without a hiss buzzing in their ears.

What we need: (A) take the six raw lesson narration takes (lesson_01 through lesson_06, WAV) and clean each one so the room hiss, hum and fan noise are gone and the host's voice sits clear and balanced — this is voice cleanup only (denoise / de-hiss), NOT music mixing or a full master; we will lay the soft sleep-music bed under it and do the final fades and loudness pass ourselves on our side. (B) Because these become the course-player lessons, we also need a transcript-and-chapter pack: a short content summary of each cleaned lesson so our LMS team can drop in captions, chapter markers, and a one-line lesson blurb for the course syllabus. (C) Deliver everything back as one tidy, organized package — the six cleaned voiceover masters in a /clean folder, the per-lesson transcript/summary text, and a short read-me recipe note listing exactly what was done to each take (so our audio person can reproduce or extend it) — all zipped into a single download. Keep the running order lesson_01 -> lesson_06. The finished audio must sound like the same calm host, just clean.

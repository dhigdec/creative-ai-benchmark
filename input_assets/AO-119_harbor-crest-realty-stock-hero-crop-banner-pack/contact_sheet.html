Full client brief
Harbor Crest Realty is a boutique waterfront-and-city brokerage whose website structure and copy already work, but the imagery feels dated and generic and no longer signals the premium, trustworthy feel we want. Refresh the visual layer into ONE cohesive, web-ready graphics family, all cut from a single hero look so the site feels intentional. (1) HERO SOURCE: source a high-quality, aspirational real-estate architecture photograph from Adobe Stock — a modern light-filled open living space or a clean contemporary home exterior at golden hour, no recognizable faces, no readable signage or brand marks — and license it at LARGE resolution (we need it big enough to cut multiple crops from, roughly 5000px on the long edge; do NOT stretch or AI-expand a small file). (2) BRAND GRADE: color-grade the licensed photo to our palette — primary deep harbor navy #17324B, warm sand accent #C9A66B, off-white #F5F1E8: auto-tone for a clean baseline, warm the white balance slightly toward the golden-hour sand tone, lift the shadows so interiors read open and airy, recover highlights so windows keep detail, and push vibrance just enough that the scene looks premium without being over-cooked. This graded file is the single MASTER every web slot is cut from. (3) RESPONSIVE HEROES (crop/reframe only, no expansion): from the graded master, produce a desktop hero 2560x1080 (~21:9 band), a tablet hero 1536x1024, and a mobile hero 1080x1350 (4:5 portrait) — each framed so the focal architecture survives the reframe. (4) SECTION HEADERS: cut two low-band section-header strips 1920x480 from the master for the 'Featured Listings' and 'About Us' sections. (5) PROMO BANNERS: cut two banner-ad sizes for our seasonal open-house campaign — a leaderboard 1200x628 and a square 1080x1080 — and give the square banner a subtle navy monochromatic treatment so it reads as the stylized promo variant rather than a plain photo. (6) LOGO: we'll supply our flat wordmark as a PNG; knock the background to transparency and vectorize it into a clean SVG so it stays razor-sharp placed over any of these graphics at any size. (7) HAND-OFF: recommend an on-brand heading/label typeface pairing for the mini style reference, and upload the finished web-ready set back to a shared Creative Cloud folder so the whole cohesive family lands in one place for my team. Deliverables: brand-graded master, 3 responsive hero crops, 2 section-header strips, 2 promo banners (leaderboard + square navy variant), 1 transparent logo SVG, a font-pairing note, and a shared CC folder. Everything must be perfectly sized to the dimensions above and feel part of one family.

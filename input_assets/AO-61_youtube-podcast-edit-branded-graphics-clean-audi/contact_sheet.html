Full client brief
You are editing one episode of a casual, fun YouTube podcast. The client supplies the raw multi-cam-ish recording (a single 1080p+ master with two speakers), their existing brand logo lockup (a flat PNG on a busy background), and a hand-shot lower-third texture plate. They want three things delivered: (A) a fully edited 1080p YouTube-ready video that flows smoothly; (B) the working hand-off so they can tweak later; and (C) any custom graphic assets you create, delivered SEPARATELY in PNG/transparent or vector format, with their logo worked in tastefully in the intro / lower-third / end screen without overpowering the content.

Concretely: produce a punchy highlight/sizzle cut from the raw footage for the cold-open, and reframe the full episode to a clean 1080p YouTube frame. Pull a short content summary to seed the chapter markers and description. For the graphics kit: lift the client's logo off its busy background to a clean transparent PNG, color-correct and brand-tint it, then vectorize it to an infinitely-scalable SVG for the end screen. Build a lower-third "name plate" from the supplied texture plate: knock out the background, fill a clean solid brand-colored bar, tint it to match, crop it to a broadcast lower-third strip. License one subtle on-brand background texture from stock for the end-screen card. The vibe is light-hearted and casual — clean and consistent branding, never stiff or corporate.

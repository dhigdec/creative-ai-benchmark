Full client brief
The Nocturne Sessions, a fictional indie synth-noir act, is releasing a new EP and needs its launch gig poster finished for print and social. The band has handed off an authored InDesign poster template (nocturne_poster_template.indd), laid out A2 portrait with a full-bleed hero-photo frame, a wordmark frame, and real InDesign data-merge fields (<<title>>, <<venue>>, <<date>>, <<support>>, <<presale>>). They also supply one raw press photo of the trio (shot on a phone in a dim rehearsal room — slightly tilted, flat/dull, cool white balance, a cluttered gear-strewn background), a flat raster promo wordmark PNG that reads "THE NOCTURNE SESSIONS," and a one-row CSV holding the show copy. The band's brief calls for "a clean modern aesthetic—nothing vintage—powered by bright, bold colours that jump off the screen and still look sharp in print," and the poster's art direction is a classic bold two-tone (duotone) gig-poster look: the hero photo rendered in just two punchy brand inks (deep indigo shadows, hot magenta highlights) rather than full colour, so it reads instantly at both A2 wall size and Instagram thumbnail size. The job: (1) prep the raw press photo — straighten the tilted frame, auto-tone the flat exposure, and push contrast hard so the two-tone mapping has clean separation between shadow and highlight; (2) convert the prepped photo to a bold duotone — first flatten it to a single-ink monochromatic base tint in the indigo shadow ink across the whole frame, then lay the hot-magenta brand ink over it as a whole-image color overlay in a lighten/screen blend so only the brighter pixels shift toward magenta, producing the two-ink poster hero; add a fine grain so the flat inks don't band on large-format print; crop it to the poster frame's ratio; (3) vectorize the flat wordmark PNG into a clean SVG so the "NOCTURNE SESSIONS" word-mark stays razor-sharp at A2 and can be reused on merch/posters exactly as the brief asks; (4) recommend a modern condensed display font pairing for the show copy so the template's type reads as clean and bold; (5) upload the duotoned hero and the vector wordmark, then run the client's authored .indd through InDesign data-merge against the event-copy CSV so the title/venue/date/support/presale bind into the template around the placed hero and wordmark; (6) export the bound poster to a print-ready A2 PDF (300 DPI, CMYK-intent) and a high-resolution JPEG for social; (7) assemble the duotoned hero, the vector wordmark, and the final poster onto a Firefly board so the band can sign off in one deep-link. All event copy stays exactly as supplied in the CSV. Deliverables: print-ready PDF, high-res JPEG, the duotone hero PNG, the wordmark SVG, and the review board link.

Difficulty comes from constructing a genuine two-tone (duotone) hero without any banned capability: the two-ink look is built by real whole-image steps (high-contrast prep -> monochromatic base tint in ink 1 -> whole-image color-overlay of ink 2 in a lighten/screen blend so only the brighter pixels shift -> grain), not by a text-to-image or generative-fill shortcut and not by any tonal-range selection the tools do not support. It then vectorizes a supplied flat wordmark, converges everything into a genuine InDesign [T] data-merge, and dual-exports.

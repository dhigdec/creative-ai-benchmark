Full client brief
Northbound Signal is a fictional independent interview podcast produced by Meridian Audio Collective — long-form conversations with founders and makers, released weekly on YouTube and the usual podcast platforms. We recorded three new episodes back-to-back and the raw mic tracks are captured separately from the cameras (a single mixed-down mono/stereo voice file per episode, roughly 28-32 minutes each). Before these go to our editor for the final cut, we need a fresh set of ears to tell us exactly what's wrong with the audio so nothing embarrassing ships. Our last drop had a whole segment where the guest's lav was clipping on every laugh and we only caught it after publish, plus one episode had an HVAC hum under the whole intro. We are NOT asking you to edit or master the episodes — that is our editor's job on the timeline. We want an audit. You will receive: (1) three raw voice recordings — ep01_raw_mix.wav (host + guest, guest mic runs hot with intermittent clipping on peaks), ep02_raw_mix.wav (steady room hiss / HVAC hum under the whole thing, low broadband noise floor), ep03_raw_mix.wav (badly unbalanced — host is loud, guest is quiet, plus two long dead-air gaps where someone stepped away); and (2) northbound_delivery_spec.pdf, our one-page delivery standard stating the targets our editor masters to (integrated loudness target -16 LUFS for the podcast feed, true-peak ceiling -1.0 dBTP, no audible hiss/hum floor above roughly -60 dB, no clipping, speaker levels within ~3 dB of each other, no dead-air gap longer than ~2 seconds in the final cut). Go through each recording end to end and hand back a Northbound Signal audio QC report that, for every issue found, gives the episode, an approximate timestamp or time-range, a plain-language description of the defect (clipping / hiss / hum / level imbalance / dead air / plosive / sibilance), which delivery-spec target it fails, and a severity (blocker / warning / nit). We also want one thing to listen to: take the worst-affected recording (ep02, the hissy one) and run a voice-only clean-up pass on it so we have a reference track that shows how clean the dialogue CAN get once the floor is removed — clearly labelled as a reference/benchmark, NOT the final master. Deliver the QC findings as a clean PDF report plus the raw findings as a structured file, with a cover summary counting issues by severity across all three episodes, and the single cleaned reference WAV alongside. Everything is voice-only; there is no music bed to consider at this stage.

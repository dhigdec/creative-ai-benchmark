Full client brief
Marlow & Vane Realty, a boutique residential agency, needs its new-listing flyer sheet finished for print and email. Our marketing lead has handed off an authored InDesign flyer template (listing_flyer_template.indd) — a US Letter portrait one-listing-per-page layout in a clean editorial style with a hero photo frame, three thumbnail frames, a crest placement, a full-bleed background frame for a subtle paper/linen texture, and REAL data-merge fields (<<address>>, <<price>>, <<beds>>, <<baths>>, <<sqft>>, <<blurb>>, <<agent_name>>, <<agent_phone>>). We are also handing off a listings roster CSV (four current new listings, one row each with those exact field names), four raw agent-shot property photos (a curb-appeal exterior, a bright living room, a kitchen, a primary bedroom), and the agency crest as a flat PNG. The photos are unappetizing as-shot: slightly tilted, dull/flat, cool white-balance, and slightly under- or over-exposed by window light. The job: (1) make every property photo look bright, warm and inviting — straighten, auto-tone, warm the white balance toward a welcoming daylight look, gently lift shadows, push vibrance a touch (never garish — this is real estate, not a burger), and crop all four consistently to the frame ratios the template expects (a 3:2 hero and matching thumbnails); (2) vectorize the flat crest PNG into a clean SVG so the monogram stays razor-sharp at large print size; (3) license a subtle paper/linen texture from Adobe Stock to back the layout without competing with the photos; (4) upload all the prepped assets, then run the client's authored .indd through InDesign data-merge against the roster CSV so every listing's address / price / beds / baths / sqft / blurb / agent contact binds into its own flyer page, with the graded photos, crest and texture placed; (5) export the bound multi-page document to a print-ready PDF (all four flyers) and also emit a high-resolution JPEG per listing for email and MLS use; (6) write a short recipe note recording the grade settings and the CSV-to-field mapping so the same treatment can be re-run next week when new listings come in. Deliverable dimensions: US Letter portrait (8.5 x 11 in), ~300 DPI print. Brand constraints: keep the editorial/clean look, do not oversaturate, crest must be crisp vector, agent contact block bound from the CSV per row.

**Template File Description:** `listing_flyer_template.indd`

This InDesign template is designed for a single-listing real estate flyer, formatted to US Letter portrait dimensions (8.5x11 inches). It features a clean, editorial aesthetic, prioritizing clear information hierarchy and visual impact for each property.

**Structure & Layout:**

*   **Background Layer:** A full-bleed image frame is positioned at the base, designated for a subtle paper or linen texture to provide a premium feel.
*   **Image Frames:**
    *   **Hero Image:** A prominent 3:2 aspect ratio image frame dominates the upper half, intended for the primary property photo.
    *   **Thumbnail Images:** Three smaller, matching image frames are arranged below the hero, suitable for additional property views.
*   **Crest Placement:** A dedicated, smaller frame is positioned for a brand or agency crest/logo, typically in the header or footer area.
*   **Listing Card:** The core content block is laid out as a distinct "listing card," designed for immediate readability.
*   **Agent Contact Footer:** A dedicated section at the bottom for agent details.

**Data Merge Placeholders (InDesign Data Merge Fields):**

The template is configured with genuine InDesign data-merge fields to facilitate automated population and pagination (one page per listing). These fields are strategically placed within the layout:

*   `<<address>>`: Positioned as the primary headline for the listing card.
*   `<<price>>`: Appears prominently in a price band beneath the address.
*   `<<beds>>`, `<<baths>>`, `<<sqft>>`: Arranged in a feature row, typically with accompanying icons or labels.
*   `<<blurb>>`: A multi-line text frame for a descriptive property overview.
*   `<<agent_name>>`: Located in the agent contact footer.
*   `<<agent_phone>>`: Paired with the agent name in the contact footer.

**Brand Styling & Typography:**

The "clean editorial" style is characterized by:

*   **Typography:** A focus on legible sans-serif and/or classic serif fonts with clear hierarchy for headlines, subheadings, and body text.
*   **Color Palette:** Likely minimal, relying on strong contrasts and possibly a single accent color for branding elements.
*   **Whitespace:** Generous use of whitespace to enhance readability and give the flyer an uncluttered, professional appearance.

Full client brief
Nordheim Atelier, an independent Scandinavian fashion label, is launching its FW26 "Concrete Season" capsule and needs a four-poster editorial series finished for its flagship windows and press kit. The look is fixed and non-negotiable: a monochrome documentary-explainer / investigative-magazine aesthetic built on grayscale cutout photography, a bold halftone print-screen texture, subtle film grain, and one signature warm-yellow ink accent — the same visual system across all four posters so they read as a set.

We are handing off: an authored InDesign poster template (poster_template.indd) laid out at A2 portrait (420x594mm) with one full-height image frame, a headline block, a look-credit block, and real data-merge fields (<<look_no>>, <<look_title>>, <<fabric_credit>>, <<stylist_credit>>); four raw runway photos shot backstage (flat, mixed venue lighting, distracting backstage clutter behind each model); our flat wordmark as a PNG; and a CSV of the four looks' titles and credits. The photos are unusable as-shot — busy backgrounds, dull flat tone, tilted horizons.

The job: (1) isolate each of the four looks cleanly off its cluttered backstage background so the garment reads as the hero on a clean plate; (2) drive every cutout to the identical editorial treatment — desaturate to grayscale, map to a two-ink duotone (near-black shadow #1A1A1A to soft paper-white highlight) via a monochromatic tint, apply the signature halftone print-screen dot texture, add fine film grain for a printed-matter feel, and warm every frame a touch toward our house yellow so none of them ever reads as cold pure-grey — the warm accent applied identically to all four; (3) crop all four consistently to the template's tall image frame; (4) vectorize the flat wordmark PNG into a clean SVG so it stays razor-sharp at A2; (5) upload all prepped assets, then run our authored .indd through InDesign data-merge against the credits CSV so each poster's look number / title / fabric + stylist credit bind into the layout with the treated image placed; (6) export each bound poster to a print-ready ~300 DPI PDF and a high-res JPEG; (7) assemble the treated images, the vector wordmark, and the four final posters onto a Firefly board so we can sign off on the treatment and the full set in one deep-link. Consistency across the four is the whole point — every step of the treatment, including the warm-yellow accent, must be applied identically to each look.

**Template Description: `poster_template.indd`**

This is an Adobe InDesign (.indd) template for a single A2 portrait (420x594mm) fashion editorial poster, styled in a monochrome documentary/magazine aesthetic.

**Structure & Layout:**
The template features a dominant full-height image frame positioned for a 4:5 aspect ratio, designed to showcase a treated runway look. Below or adjacent to this, a dedicated headline block is provided for the editorial title. A separate look-credit block is included for detailed attribution. The layout incorporates subtle thin grid-line rules, contributing to the documentary feel, and a distinct yellow highlight block as part of the editorial system's branding. A specific placeholder area is designated for the vectorized brand wordmark.

**Layers & Placeholders:**
The document is authored with live InDesign data-merge fields to facilitate automated content population. These include:
*   `<<look_no>>`
*   `<<look_title>>`
*   `<<fabric_credit>>`
*   `<<stylist_credit>>`

**Brand Styling:**
The template adheres to a monochrome palette, accented by the editorial system's signature yellow highlight block. Thin grid-line rules reinforce the structured, magazine-like, and documentary aesthetic.

Full client brief
Lumora Beauty Co. is launching "Dewdrop," a glossy translucent lip oil in a frosted-glass dropper bottle with a brushed-gold collar and cap. We only had time to photograph ONE physical sample on an iPhone against a messy studio bench, so we need you to turn that single raw shot into a full, launch-ready shade range for our Shopify PDP and Amazon listing. First, clean up the raw photo: it's slightly tilted, a touch cool/dull, and the background is cluttered. Straighten it, correct the tone and white balance so the glass reads clean and neutral, and crop it square to our 2000x2000 px listing frame. Then cut the bottle out cleanly and place it on a pure white (#FFFFFF) e-commerce background — Amazon-compliant, no drop-shadow baked in, product centered with a little margin. Next, the important part: recolor JUST the translucent oil inside the bottle into 8 shade variants for the range — Clear Gloss (the original, ungraded), Peach Nectar, Rose Quartz, Coral Crush, Berry Tint, Amber Glow, Mauve Mist, and Cherry Sheer. The tint must look like real translucent oil: internal highlights and the light passing through the glass should shift naturally with each color, NOT a flat paint fill. Critically, the brushed-gold collar, the gold cap, the dropper glass rim, and the white background/shadow must stay IDENTICAL across all 8 — only the oil changes color. Deliverables: the 8 shade variants each as a 2000x2000 px white-background JPEG PLUS a transparent-background PNG at 300 DPI (16 product files total), and a one-page variant spec sheet (PDF) listing each shade name and its hue so our packaging team can match Pantone later. Keep everything photographic and consistent — this is a color-variant set of one product, not eight different photos.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon product-recolor task: 23 connector calls, 15 distinct Adobe-server tools, well above the >=10-call / >=6-distinct-tool floor. Difficulty is in the discipline of a real color-variant job: prep one raw phone shot through a 6-step cleanup+cutout spine, fill to a compliant white background, derive ONE reusable oil-only mask via select_by_prompt, then fan that mask out across eight parallel recolors using hue-shift / single-color-saturation / color-overlay so the translucent oil changes shade while the gold collar, cap and white background stay pixel-identical -- the exact 'maintain consistent lighting, shadow, and metallic detail across all variants; translucent material highlights shift naturally' constraint from the source brief. It then produces dual deliverable formats (white JPEG + transparent PNG), a data-driven spec sheet, and a clean CC delivery folder. It respects every hard limit: image_fill_area is SOLID white fill only (no synthesized content); image_remove_background outputs only transparent PNGs; recoloring is hue/saturation/overlay manipulation of EXISTING pixels within a selection, never generative fill, never inpainting/object-removal, never text-to-image or upscaling; nothing is composed from scratch.

Why it's non-trivial
Long-horizon flagship: 19 connector calls, 13 distinct Adobe tools, spanning 2 capability groups (A1 raster/photo, plus the A4 upload/storage asset plumbing) with three independent input branches. It exercises the full headless photo pipeline (geometry -> tone -> white balance -> mask-driven selective edits -> solid-fill background cleanup -> honest multi-ratio crop/reframe export), plus isolation (remove_background) and vectorization. Difficulty comes from the masking discipline (select_subject -> invert -> fill, then a second select_by_prompt -> selective saturation on the SAME image) and from honestly re-casting a brief whose literal asks (compositing a shirt in, AI-erasing clutter) are both out of scope — the agent must recognize the [X] capabilities and deliver the in-scope equivalent. All steps are [C] headless-confirmed; no [W]/[A]/[T] dependencies, so it runs end-to-end autonomously. There is no generative step: the wide catalog banner is an honest horizontal crop of the master frame (image_crop_and_resize), which trims the top/bottom to hit the ~2:1 ratio rather than inventing canvas; the only solid-color fill is the in-scope background cleanup (step 8), and no gen-fill, canvas extension, object-removal, text-to-image, compositing, or layout-composition is used.

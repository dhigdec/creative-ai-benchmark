Creative Brief — El Vecino Cocina · Taco Tuesday + Lunch-Special Meta Ad Asset Pack

Client: El Vecino Cocina — a family-owned, authentic Mexican restaurant in West Palm Beach, FL (fictional, IP-clean). Engagement: Freelance production designer — photo + brand asset prep for a paid-social campaign. Turnaround: within one week of kickoff · two rounds of revisions included.

1. The ask

We're running 12 static Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ads across two offers — Taco Tuesday (urgent, scroll-stop, recurring promo) and Lunch Specials. Our layout team drops the final headline/CTA/offer text into the ad mockups themselves — your job is to prep the campaign-ready photo & brand assets: retouch and warm-grade our food shots, build scroll-stop variants, cut out a dish, reframe the hero to the three Meta ad sizes, license a backdrop texture, and vectorize our logo. Brand feel: warm, bold, family-oriented, community-focused — a real neighborhood favorite, not a corporate chain.

Scope note: this is asset prep, not the finished ad. No headline text or ad layout is required — deliver clean, on-brand image + brand assets the layout team can drop into their templates.

3. Art direction

  • Look: warm, bold, appetizing — lift colour temperature toward warm, boost vibrance so the salsa reds and lime greens pop, recover highlight detail on the glossy meat. Bold, not muddy; appetizing, not over-cooked/over-saturated.
  • Scroll-stop variants: the colour-splash (food-only colour) and Subject-Pop (high-contrast) versions must each read as a distinct, thumb-stopping treatment of the same hero.
  • Consistency: all hero variants and ratios come from the same graded master — one cohesive campaign look.
  • Logo: vectorize the supplied mark faithfully; do not recolour, redraw, or distort it.

4. Technical spec

  • Hero + variants + ratios: sRGB JPEG, full quality.
  • Ratios exactly 1080×1080 / 1080×1350 / 1080×1920, produced by cropping/reframing the existing shot (keep the food in frame; use the surrounding table/scene as crop margin). No pixels invented / no outpainting / no generative fill.
  • Cut-out: transparent PNG with a clean edge.
  • Logo: valid SVG, source colours preserved.
  • Backdrop texture: licensed Adobe Stock, full resolution.

5. What we're supplying (input assets — in assets/)

  • hero_taco_platter.jpg — hero al-pastor taco platter (handheld, slightly tilted, needs straighten + grade)
  • lunch_carne_asada_plate.jpg — carne-asada lunch plate on a cluttered table (this is the one to cut out)
  • margarita_chips_table.jpg — supporting margarita-and-chips scene (extra brand context for the pack; no specific deliverable is required from it)
  • restaurant_logo_flat.png — our logo (flat PNG, to vectorize)

6. Constraints (hard)

  • No generative editing — no generative fill / outpaint / text-to-image / AI object-removal / upscaling. Ratios are crop-reframed (not expanded); the cut-out is a real background removal; the logo is vectorized (not redrawn).
  • Don't invent scene pixels; keep the food in frame on every ratio.
  • Preserve the logo's exact colours and form.
  • Only the supplied photos + one licensed stock backdrop — no other third-party imagery.

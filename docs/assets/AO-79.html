**Template File Description:** `listing_flyer_template.indd`

This InDesign template is designed for a single-listing real estate flyer, formatted to US Letter portrait dimensions (8.5x11 inches). It features a clean, editorial aesthetic, prioritizing clear information hierarchy and visual impact for each property.

**Structure & Layout:**

*   **Background Layer:** A full-bleed image frame is positioned at the base, designated for a subtle paper or linen texture to provide a premium feel.
*   **Image Frames:**
    *   **Hero Image:** A prominent 3:2 aspect ratio image frame dominates the upper half, intended for the primary property photo.
    *   **Thumbnail Images:** Three smaller, matching image frames are arranged below the hero, suitable for additional property views.
*   **Crest Placement:** A dedicated, smaller frame is positioned for a brand or agency crest/logo, typically in the header or footer area.
*   **Listing Card:** The core content block is laid out as a distinct "listing card," designed for immediate readability.
*   **Agent Contact Footer:** A dedicated section at the bottom for agent details.

**Data Merge Placeholders (InDesign Data Merge Fields):**

The template is configured with genuine InDesign data-merge fields to facilitate automated population and pagination (one page per listing). These fields are strategically placed within the layout:

*   `<<address>>`: Positioned as the primary headline for the listing card.
*   `<<price>>`: Appears prominently in a price band beneath the address.
*   `<<beds>>`, `<<baths>>`, `<<sqft>>`: Arranged in a feature row, typically with accompanying icons or labels.
*   `<<blurb>>`: A multi-line text frame for a descriptive property overview.
*   `<<agent_name>>`: Located in the agent contact footer.
*   `<<agent_phone>>`: Paired with the agent name in the contact footer.

**Brand Styling & Typography:**

The "clean editorial" style is characterized by:

*   **Typography:** A focus on legible sans-serif and/or classic serif fonts with clear hierarchy for headlines, subheadings, and body text.
*   **Color Palette:** Likely minimal, relying on strong contrasts and possibly a single accent color for branding elements.
*   **Whitespace:** Generous use of whitespace to enhance readability and give the flyer an uncluttered, professional appearance.
